==================================================

Grade: 6

Subject: Portuguese

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Sixth-grade Portuguese at Colégio Horizonte shifts emphasis toward argumentation, civic discourse, and media literacy while maintaining strong reading and writing foundations. Students analyze news, opinion texts, and persuasive media, learning to distinguish fact from opinion and recognize rhetorical strategies in Brazilian and digital contexts. Workshop routines, Socratic discussion, and structured debates echo US literacy approaches to critical thinking and evidence-based communication.

Core Competencies:
- Analyze news and informational texts with awareness of author perspective and media context
- Construct and evaluate arguments using claims, evidence, and appropriate connectors
- Produce journalistic and persuasive texts for varied audiences and platforms
- Apply grammar and cohesion knowledge to strengthen clarity in formal writing

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
News and public information shape how communities understand events, and critical readers examine sources, genres, and the choices journalists make.

Topics:
- Journalism genres: news, headlines, leads, and basic report structure
- Fact versus opinion in news, editorials, and social media posts
- Comparing coverage of the same event across print, web, and video
- Partiality and framing: how word choice and selection shape meaning
- School and community news: identifying newsworthy local topics

Learning Objectives:
- Recognize that news accounts involve editorial choices and degrees of partiality
- Identify facts, participants, place, and time in news reports
- Compare how different outlets cover the same story and note differences in emphasis
- Distinguish fact from opinion in short informational and journalistic texts
- Propose relevant topics for a classroom or school news publication

BNCC Skills:
- EF06LP01, EF06LP02, EF67LP03, EF67LP04

Keywords:
- journalism
- news literacy
- fact and opinion
- media comparison
- civic reading
- framing

Project Opportunities:
- Launch a class newspaper covering Colégio Horizonte events with assigned beats
- Side-by-side analysis of Rio metro or environmental news from two Brazilian outlets
- Fake headline sorting activity: students classify statements as fact, opinion, or misleading

Assessment Suggestions:
- News analysis worksheet identifying lead, facts, and inferred audience
- Short written comparison of two articles on the same topic
- Quiz on journalism vocabulary and genre conventions

==================================================

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Argumentative texts persuade through thesis, evidence, and rhetorical choices, and writers must learn to build and critique positions with clarity and respect.

Topics:
- Argument structure: claim, reasons, evidence, and counter-argument
- Opinion genres: letters to the editor, commentaries, and short opinion articles
- Persuasive devices: word choice, metaphors, titles, and emotional appeal
- Evaluating argument strength and identifying logical gaps
- Formal register and connectors for addition, contrast, cause, and conclusion

Learning Objectives:
- Identify explicit thesis and supporting arguments in opinion texts
- Express agreement or disagreement with a published position using textual evidence
- Recognize persuasive lexical and structural choices and describe their effects
- Draft a structured opinion paragraph defending a viewpoint on a school issue
- Use conjunctions and cohesive devices to link ideas in argumentative writing

BNCC Skills:
- EF67LP05, EF67LP06, EF67LP07, EF06LP12, EF06LP03

Keywords:
- argument
- thesis
- evidence
- persuasion
- opinion writing
- rhetoric

Project Opportunities:
- Write a letter to the editor about a local issue such as beach cleanup or traffic safety
- Structured classroom debate with assigned positions and rebuttal rounds
- Advertisement analysis: compare persuasive strategies in campaigns aimed at youth

Assessment Suggestions:
- Argument mapping diagram showing claim, reasons, and evidence in a sample text
- Opinion paragraph rubric assessing thesis, support, cohesion, and tone
- Peer review checklist for identifying weak or unsupported claims

==================================================

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Multimodal media combines language, image, and sound to inform and persuade, requiring producers and consumers to understand genre conventions across platforms.

Topics:
- Visual literacy in news: photos, captions, layout, and image-text relationships
- Planning and producing news for print, audio, and video formats
- Reviews and vlogs: structure, evaluation criteria, and audience engagement
- Advertising texts: slogans, visual design, and ethical persuasion
- Interview skills: question design, active listening, and transcription basics

Learning Objectives:
- Analyze how images and layout contribute to meaning in news and media texts
- Plan a news story including research, sources, and format appropriate to medium
- Produce a short review or vlog evaluating a cultural product for peers
- Create a simple advertisement applying verbal and visual persuasive strategies
- Prepare and conduct a brief interview following a prepared question guide

BNCC Skills:
- EF67LP08, EF67LP09, EF67LP10, EF67LP11, EF67LP13

Keywords:
- multimodal
- production
- visual literacy
- interview
- advertising
- digital media

Project Opportunities:
- Produce a multimedia news package on a school event with photo and written report
- Student vlog review series of books, games, or films with editorial guidelines
- Design an awareness campaign poster on a civic theme such as water conservation

Assessment Suggestions:
- Multimedia news rubric covering research, genre conventions, and presentation
- Visual analysis paragraph explaining image-text relations in a news sample
- Interview transcript evaluation for question quality and relevance of responses

==================================================

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Grammar, civic genres, and rights-based reading integrate language study with responsible participation in school and community life.

Topics:
- Sentence structure: clauses, coordination, and basic syntactic constituents
- Agreement: nominal and verbal concord in formal writing
- Normative and legal texts: rights, rules, and school regulations
- Complaint and request letters: organization, justification, and formal tone
- Year-end synthesis: connecting argument, media, and grammar in polished writing

Learning Objectives:
- Identify clauses and coordinated periods in read and self-produced texts
- Apply nominal and verbal agreement rules in revised formal writing
- Locate rights and obligations in excerpts from the ECA and school regulations
- Analyze the structure of complaint and request letters and draft a formal example
- Edit a portfolio piece integrating argumentative structure and grammatical accuracy

BNCC Skills:
- EF06LP06, EF06LP07, EF67LP15, EF67LP17, EF06LP11

Keywords:
- grammar
- syntax
- civic writing
- formal register
- rights
- revision

Project Opportunities:
- Draft a formal request or complaint letter addressing a real school improvement
- Grammar clinic workshop where students revise news and opinion drafts in teams
- Capstone editorial board meeting to select best pieces for a published class magazine

Assessment Suggestions:
- Grammar in context test on agreement and sentence structure
- Formal letter rubric evaluating format, argument, and register
- Portfolio defense: student presents one revised text explaining authorial choices
