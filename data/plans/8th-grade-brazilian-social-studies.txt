Grade: 8

Subject: Brazilian Social Studies

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Eighth-grade Brazilian Social Studies at Colégio Horizonte focuses on contemporary Brazil from the 1990s to the present, examining economic policy, democratic participation, social inequality, and Brazil's role in a globalized world. Students study citizenship as an active practice—voting, advocacy, consumer choices, and community engagement—while analyzing how history shapes present debates on rights, environment, and development. Rio de Janeiro serves as a case study for mega-events, urban challenges, and cultural influence in national life.

Core Competencies:
- Analyze recent economic and political transformations in Brazil and their social effects
- Evaluate citizenship practices that strengthen democracy and protect rights
- Interpret media, data, and policy documents about contemporary public issues
- Connect local community concerns to national and global historical processes

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Brazil's re-democratization opened intense debate over economic models, state reform, and social justice.

Topics:
- Collor, Itamar Franco, and the Real Plan stabilization (1990s)
- Privatization, market opening, and changing role of the state
- Social programs, poverty reduction, and persistent inequality indicators
- Regional disparities: Northeast, Southeast, and Amazon development tensions
- Rio's fiscal crises and urban services in the 1990s and 2000s

Learning Objectives:
- Describe major economic policies adopted after hyperinflation
- Explain how stabilization plans affected prices, wages, and public trust
- Analyze data on inequality, education, and health across regions
- Compare arguments for state-led and market-led development strategies
- Evaluate how economic choices shape opportunities in students' communities

BNCC Skills:
- EF08HI24, EF08HI27, EF08HI34

Keywords:
- economy
- Real Plan
- inequality
- privatization
- development
- policy

Project Opportunities:
- Infographic comparing inflation and poverty indicators before and after the Real Plan
- Policy brief recommending local investment priorities with historical justification
- Interview project on how families experienced economic changes in Rio neighborhoods

Assessment Suggestions:
- Data interpretation worksheet using national economic indicators
- Short essay weighing benefits and risks of privatization in one sector
- Quiz on key terms: inflation, fiscal policy, social program, inequality

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Active citizenship requires understanding institutions, rights, and responsibilities in a diverse democracy.

Topics:
- Federal, state, and municipal government functions and elections
- 1988 Constitution rights revisited: voting age, education, health, and environment
- Political participation beyond voting: NGOs, unions, and social media advocacy
- Race, gender, and accessibility barriers to full citizenship
- Student voice: school councils, community service, and public problem-solving

Learning Objectives:
- Explain how Brazil's government branches and elections operate
- Identify civil, political, and social rights relevant to adolescents
- Analyze case studies where citizens changed policy through organized action
- Discuss obstacles marginalized groups face in accessing rights and services
- Propose a realistic citizenship project addressing a local issue

BNCC Skills:
- EF05HI04, EF05HI05, EF08HI23, EF08HI36

Keywords:
- citizenship
- constitution
- rights
- democracy
- participation
- inclusion

Project Opportunities:
- Mock municipal assembly debating a school-zone safety or environmental measure
- Rights awareness campaign for peers on voting, digital privacy, or public health
- Service-learning proposal linking Colégio Horizonte to a Rio community partner

Assessment Suggestions:
- Scenario test on resolving conflicts using constitutional rights language
- Rubric-based evaluation of citizenship project feasibility and historical grounding
- Portfolio entry reflecting on personal responsibility in a democratic society

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Globalization links Brazilian economy and culture to international markets, technology, and environmental challenges.

Topics:
- Mercosur, BRICS, and Brazil's trade partnerships
- Agricultural exports, mining, and debates over Amazon conservation
- Migration, cultural exports, and Brazil's soft power in music and sport
- Mega-events: 2007 Pan-American Games, 2014 World Cup, and 2016 Olympics in Rio
- Technology, automation, and future-of-work questions for young Brazilians

Learning Objectives:
- Describe how Brazil participates in regional and global economic networks
- Analyze environmental conflicts involving development and indigenous land rights
- Evaluate costs and benefits of hosting international mega-events
- Explain how global media shapes perceptions of Brazil abroad and at home
- Assess how globalization creates opportunities and vulnerabilities for workers

BNCC Skills:
- EF08HI32, EF08HI33, EF08HI25, EF08HI26

Keywords:
- globalization
- environment
- trade
- mega-events
- Amazon
- technology

Project Opportunities:
- Sustainability audit of a global supply chain product common in Brazil
- Rio mega-events case study with budget, infrastructure, and legacy analysis
- Podcast episode explaining Brazil's role in a current international issue

Assessment Suggestions:
- Argumentative essay on a development-versus-conservation controversy
- Map and graph activity on export partners and trade balances
- Media analysis comparing foreign and Brazilian news coverage of the same event

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Understanding Brazil's past empowers students to interpret the present and participate in shaping an equitable future.

Topics:
- Contemporary social movements: racial justice, indigenous rights, and gender equity
- Violence, public security, and historical roots of marginalization
- Disinformation, elections, and ethical use of digital platforms
- Integration review: colonial legacies through empire, republic, dictatorship, and democracy
- Capstone: student action plan grounded in historical evidence

Learning Objectives:
- Relate current social conflicts to patterns of exclusion studied in earlier grades
- Evaluate reliable sources when researching politically sensitive topics
- Explain how historical memory informs policy debates on security and rights
- Design a community-oriented action that applies social studies skills ethically
- Synthesize a personal civic stance supported by historical reasoning

BNCC Skills:
- EF08HI35, EF08HI20, EF05HI09, EF08HI36

Keywords:
- justice
- marginalization
- media literacy
- activism
- synthesis
- civic action

Project Opportunities:
- Capstone presentation: contemporary issue traced through four centuries of Brazilian history
- Fact-checking workshop on viral claims about national politics or history
- School exhibition on Rio citizens who bridged historical struggle and present leadership

Assessment Suggestions:
- Capstone rubric assessing research, historical connection, and civic proposal quality
- Source reliability test using mixed primary and secondary materials
- Final reflective essay on how Brazilian Social Studies shapes responsible citizenship
