==================================================

Grade: 5

Subject: Brazilian Social Studies

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Fifth-grade Brazilian Social Studies at Colégio Horizonte introduces students to the peoples, cultures, and landscapes that shaped Brazil before and during early colonization. Students explore indigenous societies and their relationship to territory, examine how European arrival transformed coastal regions, and compare Brazil's major geographic regions as settings for distinct ways of life. Local references to Rio de Janeiro's Guanabara Bay and Tijuca forest connect national history to the community students inhabit daily.

Core Competencies:
- Describe how indigenous, African, and European cultures contributed to Brazilian identity
- Use maps, timelines, and primary sources to organize historical information
- Explain how geography influences settlement, economy, and regional culture
- Connect citizenship, diversity, and human rights to historical examples

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Indigenous peoples developed diverse cultures deeply connected to the lands they inhabited long before European arrival.

Topics:
- First peoples of Brazil: mobility, agriculture, and fishing societies
- Indigenous ways of marking time, seasons, and oral memory
- Languages, arts, and spiritual traditions of major ethnolinguistic groups
- Territorial organization and resource use in the Amazon, Cerrado, and Atlantic Forest
- Tupinambá and other coastal groups in the Rio de Janeiro region

Learning Objectives:
- Identify indigenous peoples as original inhabitants of Brazilian territory
- Compare how different groups organized daily life according to environment
- Explain how oral tradition preserves history and cultural values
- Locate major indigenous regions on maps of Brazil
- Discuss respect for indigenous heritage as a citizenship responsibility

BNCC Skills:
- EF05HI01, EF05HI03, EF05HI08

Keywords:
- indigenous peoples
- culture
- territory
- oral history
- diversity
- Brazil

Project Opportunities:
- Illustrated map of indigenous regions with symbols for economic activities
- Oral history interview with a community elder about local place names
- Classroom museum of indigenous crafts and food plants with source cards

Assessment Suggestions:
- Map labeling quiz with short explanations of regional adaptations
- Timeline of seasonal activities for one indigenous group
- Written reflection on why indigenous memory matters today

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
European colonization reorganized power, labor, and land use along Brazil's coast and river systems.

Topics:
- Portuguese arrival and early coastal claims: Bahia, Pernambuco, and Rio de Janeiro
- Captaincies, centralized administration, and colonial government structures
- Sugar plantations, mining, and enslaved African labor in colonial economy
- Cultural exchange and conflict among indigenous, African, and European populations
- Forts, churches, and ports as markers of colonial power

Learning Objectives:
- Describe motives and methods of Portuguese colonization in Brazil
- Explain how colonial economy depended on forced labor and export crops
- Identify institutions that organized political power in colonial Brazil
- Analyze how contact changed indigenous ways of life
- Compare coastal colonial centers using maps and historical images

BNCC Skills:
- EF05HI02, EF05HI06, EF05HI09

Keywords:
- colonization
- colonial economy
- slavery
- power
- Portugal
- sources

Project Opportunities:
- Colonial trade route map linking Portugal, Africa, and Brazilian ports
- Debate cards on perspectives of indigenous leaders, settlers, and enslaved Africans
- Replica colonial document analysis: letters, inventories, and church records

Assessment Suggestions:
- Structured note-taking from a primary source image
- Short essay comparing two colonial port cities
- Multiple-choice and short-answer test on colonial institutions

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Brazil's geographic regions created distinct economies, cultures, and identities that persist today.

Topics:
- North, Northeast, Center-West, Southeast, and South: climate and landforms
- Regional economies: rubber, cattle, coffee, industry, and services
- Population distribution and migration patterns across regions
- Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo as southeastern urban and cultural centers
- Regional festivals, cuisine, music, and dialects as cultural heritage

Learning Objectives:
- Name and locate Brazil's five major regions on political and physical maps
- Associate each region with dominant economic activities and resources
- Explain how migration links regions and reshapes local culture
- Compare urban and rural life using Rio neighborhoods as examples
- Recognize regional diversity as a national strength

BNCC Skills:
- EF05HI01, EF05HI07, EF05HI10

Keywords:
- regions
- geography
- economy
- migration
- culture
- diversity

Project Opportunities:
- Regional fair with student booths presenting food, music, and economic facts
- Graph of population by region using math skills for percentages
- Travel brochure persuading visitors to explore one Brazilian region responsibly

Assessment Suggestions:
- Regional comparison chart with climate, economy, and culture columns
- Map test identifying states, capitals, and major biomes
- Presentation rubric evaluating research depth and geographic accuracy

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Citizenship and memory require recognizing whose stories are told, protected, and left out of national narratives.

Topics:
- Citizenship, rights, and responsibilities in a diverse democracy
- Material and immaterial heritage: monuments, festivals, languages, and traditions
- Memory markers in Rio: Cristo Redentor, historic downtown, and port zone
- Quilombos, indigenous lands, and Afro-Brazilian cultural patrimony
- Comparing past and present issues: land use, inclusion, and representation

Learning Objectives:
- Define citizenship and link it to respect for diversity and human rights
- Inventory local and national heritage sites with historical significance
- Analyze who is represented in monuments and public commemorations
- Compare historical and contemporary viewpoints on a current issue
- Propose ways students can protect cultural heritage in their community

BNCC Skills:
- EF05HI04, EF05HI05, EF05HI07, EF05HI09, EF05HI10

Keywords:
- citizenship
- heritage
- memory
- rights
- inclusion
- community

Project Opportunities:
- Heritage walk proposal for a Rio neighborhood with annotated map
- Campaign poster promoting respect for quilombola and indigenous rights
- End-of-year concept map linking indigenous, colonial, and regional units

Assessment Suggestions:
- Citizenship scenario responses evaluating respect and inclusion
- Heritage inventory with justification for each selected item
- Portfolio reflection on how geography and history shape identity in Brazil
