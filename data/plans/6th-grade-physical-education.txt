Grade: 6

Subject: Physical Education

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Sixth-grade physical education at Colégio Horizonte shifts from cooperative play toward structured team sports and fitness components. Students learn invasion games such as futsal and handball on the school yard, net and wall sports adapted from beach volleyball, and the five health-related fitness components through measurable activities. Tactical thinking, positional roles, and respect for rules deepen social-emotional learning through sport. Rio context surfaces in futevôlei-inspired drills, outdoor conditioning when weather permits, and discussions of community sports clubs students may join beyond school.

Core Competencies:
- Execute basic technical and tactical skills in invasion and net/wall team sports
- Identify and train health-related fitness components: cardiorespiratory endurance, muscular strength, endurance, flexibility, and body composition awareness
- Apply rules, fair play, and constructive communication during competitive team activities
- Analyze how team sports build cooperation, resilience, and inclusive participation

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Invasion team sports require coordinated movement, spatial awareness, and shared strategy to advance toward a common goal.

Topics:
- Futsal and handball: passing, receiving, dribbling, and defensive positioning
- Principles of invasion: creating space, support, and transition from defense to attack
- Rules, signals, and referee respect in small-sided games
- Warm-up progressions and injury prevention for court sports
- School yard and covered court adaptations when space is limited

Learning Objectives:
- Demonstrate accurate passing and receiving under light defensive pressure
- Maintain appropriate spacing and support during small-sided invasion games
- Explain basic rules and accept referee and coach decisions respectfully
- Apply defensive concepts including marking and intercepting passes
- Collaborate with teammates to create scoring opportunities through movement

BNCC Skills:
- EF67EF03, EF67EF04, EF67EF05

Keywords:
- invasion sports
- futsal
- teamwork
- tactics
- rules
- fair play

Project Opportunities:
- Small-sided futsal tournament with student-designed fair play awards
- Tactical diagram assignment showing player movement during a scored goal
- Research poster on a Rio community futsal club and its social role

Assessment Suggestions:
- Skills checklist for passing, dribbling, and defensive positioning
- Game observation rubric for teamwork and rule compliance
- Short written analysis of one tactical decision that changed a game

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Net and wall sports develop reaction time, anticipation, and partnership skills essential to many Carioca beach and court traditions.

Topics:
- Mini-volleyball and wall-rebound games adapted for school facilities
- Futevôlei-inspired partner drills: headers, passes, and cooperative rallies
- Serving, receiving, and court positioning in reduced teams
- Communication between partners and rotation of roles
- Beach sport culture in Rio: accessibility, etiquette, and community play

Learning Objectives:
- Perform basic serve, pass, and attack movements in net/wall formats
- Coordinate with a partner using calls and agreed positioning
- Apply modified rules for safe play on hard court and sand-style drills
- Anticipate ball trajectory and move efficiently to contact point
- Discuss how beach sports reflect Rio leisure culture and inclusive participation

BNCC Skills:
- EF67EF04, EF67EF05, EF67EF06

Keywords:
- net sports
- volleyball
- futevôlei
- partnership
- reaction time
- beach sports

Project Opportunities:
- Partner rally challenge with progressive difficulty levels
- Illustrated guide to net/wall sport rules for new students at Colégio Horizonte
- Comparison presentation: school court sports versus praia sports in Rio

Assessment Suggestions:
- Partner skill test on consecutive controlled passes or rallies
- Game performance rubric for communication and positioning
- Reflection on adapting beach sport skills to school environment

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Fitness components can be understood, measured, and improved through purposeful exercise that supports health beyond competitive sport.

Topics:
- Health-related fitness components: cardiorespiratory endurance, strength, muscular endurance, flexibility
- Fitness testing protocols adapted for school: timed runs, planks, flexibility reaches
- Circuit training using school yard equipment and bodyweight exercises
- Heart rate awareness and perceived exertion during conditioning
- Difference between exercise for health and exercise for competition

Learning Objectives:
- Define the five health-related fitness components with examples
- Participate safely in fitness assessments and record baseline results
- Design a personal circuit targeting at least three fitness components
- Monitor exertion levels and adjust intensity appropriately
- Explain how regular conditioning supports sport performance and daily health

BNCC Skills:
- EF67EF08, EF67EF09, EF67EF10

Keywords:
- fitness components
- conditioning
- endurance
- strength
- flexibility
- health

Project Opportunities:
- Personal fitness improvement plan with six-week tracking (math link for graphs)
- Class circuit station design and peer teaching rotation
- Infographic comparing exercise for health versus elite performance

Assessment Suggestions:
- Fitness portfolio with baseline and follow-up measurements
- Circuit design rubric for safety, variety, and component targeting
- Written explanation of one fitness component and how to improve it

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Social-emotional growth through sport emerges when students lead, support one another, and reflect on how physical activity shapes identity and community.

Topics:
- Student officiating and scorekeeping roles in team tournaments
- Sportsmanship under pressure: losing gracefully, encouraging opponents
- Stereotypes and inclusion in sport: gender, ability, and body type
- Proposing alternatives when preferred sports are unavailable in the community
- Year-end integration: team sports, fitness, and social responsibility

Learning Objectives:
- Officiate or support a game demonstrating knowledge of rules and fairness
- Apply strategies to manage frustration and support teammates emotionally
- Identify exclusion or bias in sport settings and propose inclusive alternatives
- Research community spaces in Rio for sport and physical activity access
- Synthesize learning across units in a personal physical education reflection

BNCC Skills:
- EF67EF06, EF67EF07, EF67EF17 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- sportsmanship
- leadership
- inclusion
- community
- reflection
- social-emotional

Project Opportunities:
- Student-run mini-tournament with officiating rotation and fair play awards
- Community sport access map of neighborhoods near Colégio Horizonte
- Panel discussion on stereotypes in sport with proposed school action steps

Assessment Suggestions:
- Officiating and leadership observation rubric during tournament
- Reflective essay on personal social-emotional growth through team sports
- End-of-year portfolio combining fitness data, tactical work, and fair play evidence
