==================================================

Grade: 7

Subject: English

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Seventh-grade English at Colégio Horizonte expands literary study across short stories, poetry, and essays. Students analyze form and content in diverse genres, write with increasing sophistication, and engage in structured academic discourse. Near-native proficiency is expected in classroom interaction, and texts include Brazilian authors writing in English alongside canonical and contemporary voices from around the world.

Core Competencies:
- Analyze short fiction for structure, theme, and authorial choices
- Read and interpret poetry using sound, imagery, and figurative language
- Write expository and personal essays with thesis, support, and cohesion
- Infer global meaning from parts of a text and articulate interpretations orally

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Short stories compress narrative craft, demanding careful attention to structure, irony, and theme.

Topics:
- Short story elements: exposition, complication, climax, and denouement in compact form
- Unreliable narrators and surprise endings
- Irony: verbal, situational, and dramatic
- Anthology reading: diverse authors, settings, and historical periods
- Socratic seminars on open-ended interpretive questions

Learning Objectives:
- Plot short story structure and label key moments with evidence
- Identify irony and explain how it shapes reader understanding
- Formulate interpretive questions that have more than one valid answer
- Support claims with precise quotations and line references
- Compare two short stories on theme or narrative technique

BNCC Skills:
- EF07LI06, EF07LI07, EF07LI08, EF07LI09, EF07LI11

Keywords:
- short stories
- plot structure
- irony
- theme
- seminar
- interpretation

Project Opportunities:
- Short story map gallery with annotated plot diagrams
- Irony hunt presentation across three anthology selections
- Creative short story writing mimicking a studied author's structural choices

Assessment Suggestions:
- Short story analysis essay with thesis and embedded evidence
- Socratic seminar rubric for preparation, participation, and citation
- Comparative paragraph on theme across two stories

==================================================

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Poetry communicates through condensed language, sound patterns, and layered meaning.

Topics:
- Poetic forms: free verse, sonnet introduction, haiku, and spoken word
- Sound devices: rhyme, rhythm, alliteration, and assonance
- Figurative language in poetry: extended metaphor and symbolism
- Visual and concrete poetry; poems about place, including Rio landscapes
- Performance poetry and oral interpretation with expression and pacing

Learning Objectives:
- Scan a poem for sound devices and describe their contribution to mood
- Interpret figurative language and symbolism in multiple readings
- Compare how form constraints shape a poet's choices
- Perform a poem with attention to breath, emphasis, and audience
- Write original poems using studied techniques and deliberate revision

BNCC Skills:
- EF07LI05, EF07LI10, EF07LI15, EF07LI17, EF07LI18

Keywords:
- poetry
- sound devices
- figurative language
- performance
- form
- creative writing

Project Opportunities:
- Poetry anthology: class collection with author notes on craft choices
- Rio place poem project: field observations translated into sensory verse
- Poetry slam or recitation event with optional Portuguese-English bilingual piece

Assessment Suggestions:
- Poetic device analysis worksheet with short written responses
- Original poem portfolio with reflection on revisions
- Oral recitation evaluation for memorization, expression, and interpretation

==================================================

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Essays organize ideas to inform, reflect, or persuade through clear structure and developed support.

Topics:
- Essay types: personal narrative essay, expository essay, and compare-contrast
- Thesis statements and topic sentences
- Paragraph unity, transitions, and cohesive devices
- Research-lite essays: integrating one or two credible sources
- Peer review workshops and revision for clarity and academic tone

Learning Objectives:
- Draft thesis statements that answer a prompt with a debatable or focused claim
- Organize essays into introduction, body paragraphs, and conclusion
- Use transitions to connect ideas within and between paragraphs
- Integrate a source with basic attribution and paraphrase
- Revise essays for organization, word choice, and convention

BNCC Skills:
- EF07LI12, EF07LI13, EF07LI14, EF07LI19, EF07LI20

Keywords:
- essay writing
- thesis
- expository
- revision
- cohesion
- research

Project Opportunities:
- Personal essay on identity as a bilingual student in Rio
- Compare-contrast essay on two poems or short stories from the year
- Peer editing conference with structured feedback protocol

Assessment Suggestions:
- Expository essay rubric: thesis, organization, evidence, and conventions
- Thesis statement workshop assessment with revision trail
- Source integration check: one paragraph with proper introduction of evidence

==================================================

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Confident readers and writers connect genre study to intercultural understanding and academic independence.

Topics:
- Multi-genre synthesis: linking stories, poems, and essays on shared themes
- Intercultural competence: English varieties, code-switching, and representation in texts
- Independent reading project with analytical presentation
- Digital literacy: selecting reliable online texts in English
- Year-end capstone: portfolio and reflective letter on literary growth

Learning Objectives:
- Synthesize ideas across genres in a thematic capstone project
- Discuss how English functions in Brazil and globally without deficit framing
- Select and analyze an independent reading text with minimal scaffolding
- Evaluate online sources for credibility before use in school work
- Articulate personal growth as a reader, writer, and discussant in English

BNCC Skills:
- EF07LI21, EF07LI22, EF07LI23 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- synthesis
- intercultural
- independent reading
- digital literacy
- capstone
- reflection

Project Opportunities:
- Thematic capstone board linking one story, one poem, and one essay
- Independent reading TED-style talk with visual support
- Digital source evaluation guide created for classmates

Assessment Suggestions:
- Capstone project rubric evaluating cross-genre connections and evidence
- Independent reading presentation with Q&A
- Reflective letter assessed for specificity and forward-looking goals
