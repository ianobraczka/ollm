==================================================

Grade: 6

Subject: English

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Sixth-grade English at Colégio Horizonte introduces literary analysis through sustained study of novel excerpts and connected nonfiction. Students read closely, annotate passages, and discuss how authors develop characters, setting, and conflict across longer texts. The program maintains near-native expectations for oral and written English while aligning with BNCC Língua Inglesa skills and weaving Brazilian perspectives into comparisons with international literature.

Core Competencies:
- Read extended fiction excerpts with annotation and evidence-based discussion
- Identify and explain introductory literary devices: metaphor, simile, and symbolism
- Write structured literary paragraphs with topic sentences and textual evidence
- Present oral interpretations of texts with clarity and appropriate academic register

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Close reading of novel excerpts reveals how authors open stories and establish voice, setting, and tension.

Topics:
- Novel excerpt study: first chapters and pivotal scenes from age-appropriate texts
- Annotation strategies: margin notes, sticky flags, and question marks
- Setting and atmosphere: how place shapes mood and character experience
- Point of view: first person vs. third person limited introduction

Learning Objectives:
- Annotate a passage to track questions, predictions, and important details
- Describe how an opening chapter hooks the reader and introduces conflict
- Explain the effect of setting on character choices using text evidence
- Distinguish first-person and third-person narration in sample excerpts
- Discuss readings in Socratic-style circles with prepared talking points

BNCC Skills:
- EF06LI07, EF06LI08, EF06LI09, EF06LI11, EF06LI12

Keywords:
- close reading
- novel excerpts
- annotation
- setting
- point of view
- evidence

Project Opportunities:
- Annotated excerpt portfolio with teacher-modeled and student-created notes
- Setting illustration project: visual mood board paired with quoted descriptions
- Book club launch: groups choose from curated excerpt sets for quarter-long study

Assessment Suggestions:
- Annotated passage evaluation using quality-of-notes rubric
- Short analytical paragraph on how setting affects a character
- Literature circle discussion grade with self-assessment of preparation

==================================================

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Literary devices are deliberate choices that shape meaning and reader response.

Topics:
- Figurative language: simile, metaphor, personification, and hyperbole
- Imagery and sensory language in descriptive passages
- Introductory symbolism: objects, colors, and recurring motifs
- Comparing craft across two excerpts from different authors or cultures

Learning Objectives:
- Identify similes and metaphors and explain their effect on meaning
- Analyze a descriptive passage for imagery and emotional impact
- Propose plausible symbolic readings supported by textual patterns
- Compare how two authors describe a similar experience using different devices
- Use new vocabulary accurately in discussion and written response

BNCC Skills:
- EF06LI04, EF06LI10, EF06LI17, EF06LI18, EF06LI19

Keywords:
- figurative language
- imagery
- symbolism
- literary devices
- comparison
- vocabulary

Project Opportunities:
- Figurative language scavenger hunt across class novel excerpts
- Found poem created entirely from lines in a studied text
- Device comparison essay: two authors describing nature, city, or home

Assessment Suggestions:
- Literary devices identification test with short effect explanations
- Passage analysis: underline devices and write a paragraph on overall impact
- Vocabulary-in-context assessment drawn from unit readings

==================================================

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Character development and conflict drive narrative and invite readers to interpret motive and change.

Topics:
- Character types: protagonist, antagonist, foil, and supporting cast
- Internal vs. external conflict
- Character arcs: static and dynamic characters across excerpt sequences
- Dialogue as characterization: speech patterns, subtext, and relationships

Learning Objectives:
- Map character relationships and trace change across multiple chapters
- Classify conflicts as internal or external with examples from the text
- Analyze dialogue to infer traits, relationships, and unstated feelings
- Connect fiction themes to paired nonfiction for broader context
- Write a character analysis paragraph with embedded quotations

BNCC Skills:
- EF06LI05, EF06LI13, EF06LI14, EF06LI15, EF06LI22

Keywords:
- character analysis
- conflict
- dialogue
- character arc
- nonfiction pairing
- interpretation

Project Opportunities:
- Character relationship web with quoted evidence for each connection
- Internal vs. external conflict chart applied to two class texts
- Author interview simulation: students research and role-play Q&A in English

Assessment Suggestions:
- Character analysis essay with introduction, body, and conclusion
- Conflict classification quiz with justification paragraphs
- Paired-text response: how nonfiction deepens understanding of fiction

==================================================

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Emerging literary analysts synthesize reading, writing, and cultural reflection into confident academic expression.

Topics:
- Literary paragraph structure: claim, evidence, analysis, and conclusion
- Introduction to essay organization beyond single paragraphs
- Cross-cultural reading: texts from Brazil, the UK, the US, and other English-speaking contexts
- Presentation skills: quoting texts, eye contact, and responding to audience questions

Learning Objectives:
- Write multi-paragraph literary responses with clear thesis and organization
- Integrate quotations smoothly with lead-ins and citation of page or line
- Compare cultural contexts in texts without stereotyping or oversimplifying
- Deliver a five-minute oral interpretation supported by visual aids or excerpts
- Reflect on growth as a reader and writer across the academic year

BNCC Skills:
- EF06LI06, EF06LI24, EF06LI25, EF06LI26 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- literary analysis
- essay structure
- intercultural
- presentation
- synthesis
- portfolio

Project Opportunities:
- Cross-cultural text comparison project: Rio youth experience vs. global narratives
- Oral interpretation festival with peer feedback forms
- End-of-year literary portfolio with cover letter to seventh-grade teacher

Assessment Suggestions:
- Multi-paragraph literary essay on a studied excerpt with rubric
- Oral presentation evaluation for content, delivery, and use of evidence
- Portfolio review conference with student-led reflection on goals met
