==================================================

Grade: 5

Subject: Physical Education

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Fifth-grade physical education at Colégio Horizonte builds confident movers through locomotor skills, cooperative games, and daily health habits. Students explore running, jumping, hopping, and directional changes on the school yard and covered court, learning to move safely in shared spaces. Cooperative games and Brazilian playground traditions develop teamwork, fair play, and respect for diverse abilities. Light Rio context appears through adapted beach games such as frescobol and circle relays that echo Carioca street and praia culture while staying practical for school facilities.

Core Competencies:
- Demonstrate fundamental locomotor and non-locomotor skills with control and spatial awareness
- Participate in cooperative games using communication, strategy, and inclusive teamwork
- Apply basic health habits linking movement, hydration, rest, and personal hygiene
- Reflect on fair play, respect for peers, and emotional regulation during physical activity

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Locomotor skills are the foundation for all movement; practicing them with control builds confidence and body awareness.

Topics:
- Locomotor patterns: walking, running, galloping, skipping, hopping, jumping, leaping
- Directional and level changes: forward, backward, sideways, high and low pathways
- Balance, landing, and stopping with control on varied surfaces
- Spatial awareness: personal space, general space, and safe movement around others
- Warm-up and cool-down routines for school yard sessions

Learning Objectives:
- Perform locomotor skills with appropriate speed, rhythm, and body control
- Travel through space using different pathways without colliding with peers
- Land from jumps with bent knees and balanced posture
- Follow teacher signals for starting, stopping, and changing direction
- Describe how warming up prepares muscles and joints for activity

BNCC Skills:
- EF35EF01, EF35EF02, EF35EF07

Keywords:
- locomotor skills
- movement
- balance
- spatial awareness
- coordination
- body control

Project Opportunities:
- Locomotor obstacle course designed by student pairs on the school yard
- Movement vocabulary poster with student-drawn action poses
- "Travel Around Rio" relay using landmarks as stations (math link for distance estimation)

Assessment Suggestions:
- Locomotor skills checklist with peer and teacher observation
- Short reflection on one skill improved during the quarter
- Safety and spatial awareness rubric during group movement activities

==================================================

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Cooperative games teach that success in movement depends on communication, shared goals, and respect for every participant.

Topics:
- Brazilian and international playground games: cabo de guerra adaptado, amarelinha, dodgeball variations
- Team-building challenges: parachute games, group balances, and trust activities
- Turn-taking, role rotation, and inclusive rule modifications
- Frescobol and hand-eye cooperative drills adapted for school court
- Conflict resolution and fair play during competitive play

Learning Objectives:
- Participate actively in cooperative games while encouraging teammates
- Propose fair rule changes so all students can contribute meaningfully
- Use verbal and non-verbal communication to coordinate group movement
- Recognize when competition becomes unfair and suggest restorative solutions
- Value diverse strengths within a group during physical challenges

BNCC Skills:
- EF35EF03, EF35EF04, EF35EF12

Keywords:
- cooperative games
- teamwork
- fair play
- communication
- inclusion
- conflict resolution

Project Opportunities:
- Student-designed cooperative game taught to another class section
- Fair play charter displayed in the gym with signed class commitments
- Video or illustrated guide to a traditional Brazilian playground game

Assessment Suggestions:
- Teacher observation rubric for cooperation and sportsmanship
- Group debrief on one challenge solved through teamwork
- Peer recognition notes for inclusive behavior during games

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Healthy habits formed in childhood—regular activity, balanced nutrition, hydration, and rest—support lifelong physical and emotional well-being.

Topics:
- Benefits of daily physical activity for heart, muscles, and mood
- Food groups, hydration, and energy for play and learning
- Sleep, screen-time balance, and recovery after exercise
- Personal hygiene: sweat, sunscreen awareness, and care after activity
- Sedentary behavior awareness and simple alternatives at home and school

Learning Objectives:
- Explain how regular movement supports physical and mental health
- Identify healthy snack and hydration choices before and after exercise
- Describe a balanced day including activity, rest, and study time
- Practice post-activity hygiene routines consistently
- Set one realistic personal health habit goal and track progress weekly

BNCC Skills:
- EF35EF08, EF35EF09, EF35EF11

Keywords:
- health habits
- nutrition
- hydration
- rest
- hygiene
- wellbeing

Project Opportunities:
- Weekly activity and hydration log with simple bar graph (math link)
- Healthy habits brochure for younger students at Colégio Horizonte
- Class poster on "moving more, sitting less" with Rio outdoor activity ideas

Assessment Suggestions:
- Health habit tracker review with goal-setting conference
- Short written response linking nutrition choices to energy during PE
- Quiz on hygiene and recovery practices after physical activity

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Integrating movement skills, cooperative play, and health knowledge empowers students to enjoy active living as a lifelong practice.

Topics:
- Introduction to game versus sport: rules, equipment, and community contexts
- Modified invasion and net games: futsal basics, mini-volleyball on school court
- Gymnastics general elements: balances, rolls, and simple group sequences
- Year-end fitness festival combining locomotor stations and cooperative challenges
- Reflection on personal growth in movement, teamwork, and health awareness

Learning Objectives:
- Distinguish informal games from organized sports using BNCC criteria
- Apply locomotor and cooperative skills in modified sport activities
- Perform basic gymnastics elements safely in small group routines
- Demonstrate fair play and emotional control during festival competitions
- Articulate personal physical education goals for sixth grade

BNCC Skills:
- EF35EF05, EF35EF06, EF35EF07 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- games
- sports
- gymnastics
- integration
- fair play
- active living

Project Opportunities:
- End-of-year movement festival planned and led by student committees
- Group gymnastics sequence performed for parents or younger grades
- Personal active-living action plan for summer including praia and park options in Rio

Assessment Suggestions:
- Festival participation rubric covering skills, cooperation, and safety
- Written self-assessment on growth across all four units
- Teacher conference using portfolio of health habit tracking and game reflections
