Grade: 5

Subject: Portuguese

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Fifth-grade Portuguese at Colégio Horizonte strengthens reading fluency, narrative comprehension, and confident oral and written expression in Brazilian Portuguese. Students read and produce fiction and everyday texts, building on workshop-style routines influenced by US literacy practices such as read-alouds, reader response journals, and peer conferencing. Shared reading of Brazilian children's literature and occasional references to Rio de Janeiro settings anchor language learning in familiar cultural contexts.

Core Competencies:
- Read literary and informational texts with increasing autonomy, accuracy, and expression
- Identify and use narrative elements including plot, character, setting, and dialogue
- Write organized paragraphs and short texts with appropriate cohesion and conventions
- Participate in discussions and presentations that respect diverse viewpoints

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Readers build fluency and deep comprehension by engaging with narrative structure, dialogue, and the sounds and rhythms of literary language.

Topics:
- Independent and shared reading of short stories, fables, and Brazilian children's novels
- Narrative elements: exposition, rising action, climax, resolution, narrator, and point of view
- Dialogue and speech verbs: direct and indirect discourse in fiction
- Poetry appreciation: rhyme, rhythm, stanzas, and refrains in Brazilian verse

Learning Objectives:
- Read grade-level literary texts aloud and silently with appropriate fluency and intonation
- Identify plot sequence, main characters, setting, and narrator in fiction
- Explain how dialogue and speech verbs reveal character traits and advance the story
- Recognize sound and rhythm devices in poems and describe their effect on meaning
- Maintain a reader response journal with predictions, connections, and questions

BNCC Skills:
- EF35LP21, EF35LP22, EF35LP23, EF35LP26

Keywords:
- narrative
- fluency
- dialogue
- poetry
- comprehension
- reader response

Project Opportunities:
- Classroom read-aloud festival featuring Brazilian short stories with student reflections
- Illustrated story map poster tracing plot events and character relationships
- Poetry performance circle with memorized verses from Machado de Assis adaptations or cordel excerpts

Assessment Suggestions:
- Oral reading fluency rubric assessing accuracy, rate, and expression
- Written response explaining how a character changes across a story
- Poetry annotation identifying rhyme scheme and one effect of sound repetition

==================================================

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Writers craft engaging narratives by combining descriptive detail, sequenced events, and cohesive language that guides the reader through time and space.

Topics:
- Planning fiction: brainstorming, story arcs, and graphic organizers
- Descriptive language: adjectives, sensory details, and figurative comparisons
- Dialogue formatting, speech tags, and paragraph structure in narratives
- Cohesion: pronouns, temporal connectors, and logical sequencing of events

Learning Objectives:
- Plan and draft a short fictional narrative with a clear beginning, middle, and end
- Use descriptive details and dialogue to develop characters and setting
- Apply pronoun reference and temporal connectors to maintain text cohesion
- Distinguish direct and indirect speech and use speech verbs appropriately
- Revise drafts using peer feedback and a checklist focused on clarity and conventions

BNCC Skills:
- EF35LP25, EF35LP27, EF35LP30, EF05LP26, EF05LP07

Keywords:
- writing workshop
- narrative craft
- cohesion
- revision
- description
- dialogue

Project Opportunities:
- Personal narrative anthology: each student contributes a story set in a Rio neighborhood or school life
- Partner revision conference with structured feedback forms modeled on writing workshop
- Digital ministory with images and text exploring ciberpoem and multimodal formats

Assessment Suggestions:
- Narrative writing rubric evaluating plot, character, cohesion, and conventions
- Mid-process conference notes documenting revision decisions
- Short grammar application exercise on verb agreement and connector use in student drafts

==================================================

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Everyday texts serve specific purposes in daily life, and readers and writers must recognize genre conventions to communicate clearly in real situations.

Topics:
- Instructional texts: game rules, recipes, and how-to guides
- Humor genres: jokes, anecdotes, cartoons, and their situational context
- Letters and personal writing: format, audience, and purpose
- Oral presentation of opinions on school and community topics

Learning Objectives:
- Read and follow instructional texts identifying imperative verbs and step sequences
- Produce clear instructional writing for a peer audience such as a classroom game
- Recognize humor conventions and create appropriate short humorous texts
- Deliver a brief oral argument on a topic of interest respecting different viewpoints
- Analyze how images, tone, and word choice work together in digital reviews

BNCC Skills:
- EF05LP09, EF05LP10, EF05LP11, EF05LP12, EF05LP19

Keywords:
- genre
- instructions
- audience
- oral expression
- humor
- everyday texts

Project Opportunities:
- Design and write rules for a new playground or classroom game with illustrated guide
- Record a short vlog review of a book or film popular among fifth graders
- Class debate on a school issue such as recycling or library hours with turn-taking norms

Assessment Suggestions:
- Instructional text evaluation for clarity, sequence, and imperative verb use
- Oral presentation rubric for content, organization, and respectful interaction
- Genre comparison worksheet distinguishing instructional and humorous text features

==================================================

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Research, reference tools, and critical reading of information prepare students to learn across subjects and navigate media with growing independence.

Topics:
- Dictionary and reference skills: entries, abbreviations, and word meanings in context
- Research process: questions, sources, notes, and organized presentation of findings
- Reading graphs, tables, and informational texts across curricular areas
- Comparing news and information across media for reliability

Learning Objectives:
- Use dictionary entries to clarify word meaning, spelling, and grammatical information
- Conduct a short research project using print and digital sources with teacher guidance
- Organize findings in a text with images, tables, or simple graphs when appropriate
- Compare two sources on the same topic and explain which appears more reliable
- Identify multimodal resources in digital literary texts and discuss their effects

BNCC Skills:
- EF05LP22, EF05LP24, EF05LP25, EF05LP16, EF05LP28

Keywords:
- research
- reference
- media literacy
- information
- digital texts
- integration

Project Opportunities:
- Encyclopedia or dictionary entry on a Rio landmark or local ecosystem (science link)
- Mini research fair with poster presentations and peer note-taking
- End-of-year portfolio showcasing narrative, instructional, and research writing samples

Assessment Suggestions:
- Research report rubric covering question, sources, organization, and conventions
- Dictionary skills quiz using authentic entry formats
- End-of-year reading and writing portfolio review with student self-reflection
