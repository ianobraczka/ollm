==================================================

Grade: 7

Subject: Portuguese

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Seventh-grade Portuguese at Colégio Horizonte deepens literary analysis, academic research, and stylistic awareness across fiction, drama, and nonfiction. Students read with attention to figurative language, narrative voice, and intertextual connections while conducting structured inquiries that culminate in summaries, presentations, and analytical writing. Methods drawn from US literacy frameworks—close reading, text-dependent questions, and annotated bibliographies—support BNCC expectations for the artistic-literary and study-and-research fields.

Core Competencies:
- Analyze literary texts identifying structure, figurative language, and authorial choices
- Conduct research using varied sources, notes, and ethical citation practices
- Write expository and analytical texts with coherent progression and formal conventions
- Participate in academic discussion demonstrating evidence-based reasoning and turn-taking

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Literary texts construct meaning through form, voice, and intertextual dialogue, and analytical readers interpret effects rather than retelling plot alone.

Topics:
- Short fiction and chronicle: theme, conflict, and narrative perspective
- Drama: acts, scenes, stage directions, and performance as interpretation
- Figurative language: metaphor, comparison, personification, and hyperbole
- Relationships among literature, music, and visual arts in Brazilian culture

Learning Objectives:
- Read literary texts independently selecting appropriate comprehension strategies
- Identify dramatic structure including characters, dialogue, and stage directions
- Analyze how figurative language creates effects of meaning in poetry and prose
- Compare a literary text with another artistic manifestation and explain connections
- Support an interpretive claim about a text with specific textual evidence

BNCC Skills:
- EF67LP27, EF67LP28, EF67LP29, EF67LP38, EF07LP14

Keywords:
- literary analysis
- figurative language
- drama
- close reading
- interpretation
- intertextuality

Project Opportunities:
- Scene study and staged reading of a Brazilian play excerpt with director's notes
- Figurative language gallery walk analyzing metaphors in poems by Bandeira or Drummond
- Comparative essay linking a short story setting to a song or artwork from Rio's cultural scene

Assessment Suggestions:
- Analytical paragraph rubric assessing claim, evidence, and explanation of effect
- Drama structure quiz identifying genre markers in a script excerpt
- Oral interpretation presenting one literary passage with justified reading choices

==================================================

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Research is a disciplined inquiry process requiring focused questions, credible sources, organized notes, and clear communication of findings.

Topics:
- Research design: topic narrowing, guiding questions, and search strategies
- Source evaluation: credibility, bias, date, and cross-referencing
- Note-taking formats: outlines, summaries, and quotation management
- Academic summaries and encyclopedia-style entries with proper structure

Learning Objectives:
- Plan a research project with defined scope and investigable questions
- Select and evaluate sources from print and open digital collections
- Take structured notes distinguishing paraphrase, summary, and direct quotation
- Produce a summary or short encyclopedic entry organizing findings clearly
- Recognize hypertext structures in informational texts and navigate them effectively

BNCC Skills:
- EF67LP20, EF67LP21, EF67LP22, EF67LP25, EF67LP26

Keywords:
- research
- sources
- note-taking
- summary
- inquiry
- academic writing

Project Opportunities:
- Thematic research project on Brazilian biodiversity or urban history with oral presentation
- Collaborative hypertext mini-site on a science or social studies topic (cross-curricular link)
- Source reliability trial: teams defend or challenge assigned websites using criteria

Assessment Suggestions:
- Research plan evaluation including questions, source list, and timeline
- Summary rubric assessing fidelity to source, organization, and formal conventions
- Presentation rubric for clarity, use of notes, and response to audience questions

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Syntax, cohesion, and modality refine written expression, enabling students to control tone, precision, and logical progression in academic texts.

Topics:
- Sentence analysis: subject, predicate, complements, and adverbial expansion
- Transitive and intransitive verbs; direct and indirect objects
- Cohesive devices: anaphoric pronouns, lexical substitution, and sequencing
- Modality and argumentativity: hedging, emphasis, and evaluative language

Learning Objectives:
- Identify basic clause structure and verbal complements in complex sentences
- Use cohesive reference and connectors to improve continuity across paragraphs
- Recognize modal and argumentative markers and explain their effects on tone
- Form derived words using productive prefixes and suffixes in written production
- Revise expository drafts applying grammar and cohesion knowledge systematically

BNCC Skills:
- EF07LP04, EF07LP05, EF07LP07, EF07LP12, EF07LP13

Keywords:
- syntax
- cohesion
- modality
- morphology
- revision
- expository writing

Project Opportunities:
- Sentence surgery workshop: expand and combine sentences in research summaries
- Cohesion editing relay where teams improve a poorly connected sample essay
- Word study project tracing prefix and suffix families across subject-area vocabulary

Assessment Suggestions:
- Syntax identification test using sentences from student and literary texts
- Revised expository paragraph scored for cohesion and grammatical accuracy
- Short analysis explaining how modality shifts meaning in two opinion excerpts

==================================================

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Creative production and scholarly habits converge when students write fiction and poetry with craft awareness while presenting research with academic integrity.

Topics:
- Fiction and suspense narratives: pacing, point of view, and descriptive control
- Fixed and free verse: sonnets, quatrains, and visual or digital poetry forms
- Oral discussion norms: turn-taking, note-taking from lectures, and academic dialogue
- Topical organization in expository writing: general to specific and problem-solution

Learning Objectives:
- Create a short fictional narrative applying learned craft elements and cohesion
- Compose poems using versification resources and deliberate sound or visual effects
- Respect discussion turns and take useful notes during presentations and debates
- Organize an expository text using recognized topical patterns for clarity
- Reflect on growth as reader, writer, and researcher in a year-end portfolio statement

BNCC Skills:
- EF67LP30, EF67LP31, EF67LP23, EF67LP24, EF07LP10

Keywords:
- creative writing
- poetry
- discussion
- portfolio
- organization
- synthesis

Project Opportunities:
- Literary magazine featuring student fiction, poetry, and research summaries
- Poetry slam or declamation event with original and canonical Brazilian works
- Capstone presentation synthesizing one research thread and one creative piece

Assessment Suggestions:
- Creative writing rubric balancing craft, cohesion, and conventions
- Portfolio review conference with student-led reflection on two selected pieces
- Discussion participation rubric assessing preparation, evidence use, and collegiality
