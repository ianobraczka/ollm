Grade: 7

Subject: Physical Education

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Seventh-grade physical education at Colégio Horizonte emphasizes athletics, nutrition for active bodies, and deeper conditioning. Students experience track and field events on the school yard, analyze how food fuels performance and recovery, and train speed, power, and endurance through structured programs. Social-emotional learning continues through team relays, peer coaching, and discussions of body image and health messaging. Rio context includes references to municipal athletics programs, outdoor training in parks, and the role of beach running and functional fitness in Carioca active culture.

Core Competencies:
- Demonstrate technique and safety in athletics events including running, jumping, and throwing
- Analyze the relationship between nutrition, hydration, and physical performance
- Apply training principles to improve speed, power, endurance, and flexibility
- Evaluate media messages about health, performance, and body image with critical awareness

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Athletics develops measurable motor skills—speed, power, coordination, and endurance—through events that reward technique and personal progress.

Topics:
- Sprinting mechanics: start, acceleration, and relay exchanges
- Middle-distance running and pacing strategies on school track or yard
- Jumping events: standing long jump, vertical jump, and approach fundamentals
- Throwing events: shot put and javelin-style throws with modified equipment

Learning Objectives:
- Demonstrate proper sprint start and relay baton exchange in practice
- Apply pacing strategy appropriate to middle-distance runs
- Execute jumping and throwing techniques with safe body alignment
- Measure and record personal performance data across multiple attempts
- Explain how athletics events test different motor abilities and fitness components

BNCC Skills:
- EF67EF03, EF67EF04, EF67EF08

Keywords:
- athletics
- track and field
- sprinting
- jumping
- throwing
- personal best

Project Opportunities:
- School yard athletics meet with student officials and record keepers (math link)
- Personal best progression chart across three events over the quarter
- Research on Brazilian athletics heroes and their training habits

Assessment Suggestions:
- Event technique rubric for running, jumping, and throwing
- Performance log with analysis of improvement strategies
- Safety and rules quiz before competitive athletics day

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Nutrition and hydration directly affect energy, recovery, and performance; informed choices support both athletic goals and long-term health.

Topics:
- Macronutrients and micronutrients for active adolescents
- Pre-activity, during-activity, and post-activity fueling strategies
- Hydration, electrolyte balance, and heat considerations in Rio climate
- Reading labels and identifying misleading health claims on products

Learning Objectives:
- Describe how carbohydrates, proteins, and fats support activity and recovery
- Plan a balanced pre- and post-exercise meal or snack for a training day
- Explain dehydration risks and appropriate hydration habits during exercise
- Critically evaluate food marketing aimed at young athletes
- Recognize signs of unhealthy eating patterns and identify trusted adults for support

BNCC Skills:
- EF67EF08, EF67EF09, EF67EF10

Keywords:

Project Opportunities:

Assessment Suggestions:
- Nutrition case study: analyze a student's training day food choices
- Written plan for hydration during a hot-weather school yard session
- Critical media analysis of one advertisement targeting young athletes

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Structured conditioning programs build the physical capacities athletics and daily life demand while teaching students to train safely and progressively.

Topics:
- Speed and agility drills: ladder work, cone courses, and change of direction
- Plyometric and power development with age-appropriate progressions
- Muscular endurance circuits and core stability for athletic performance
- Flexibility and mobility routines for injury prevention

Learning Objectives:
- Perform speed and agility drills with correct form and effort control
- Execute plyometric and strength-endurance exercises using safe progressions
- Design a balanced conditioning session targeting multiple fitness components
- Explain how flexibility and mobility reduce injury risk in athletics
- Collaborate on class agreements that ensure all students can participate safely

Assessment Suggestions:
- Conditioning session plan evaluated for balance, safety, and progression
- Practical test on agility and core stability stations
- Peer evaluation of inclusive behavior during high-intensity training

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Athletics, nutrition, and conditioning converge when students take ownership of their training, support peers, and pursue active living beyond competition.

Topics:
- Combined athletics and conditioning preparation for year-end meet
- Peer coaching: feedback, encouragement, and constructive correction
- Urban adventure activities: parkour basics, orienteering, and safe risk assessment
- Brazilian martial arts introduction: capoeira movements and cultural context

Learning Objectives:
- Prepare for athletics competition using nutrition and conditioning knowledge
- Provide respectful peer feedback on technique during practice
- Assess risks and safety procedures for urban adventure activities
- Participate in capoeira-inspired movement respecting cultural origins
- Propose personal active-living goals connecting school learning to Rio community options

BNCC Skills:
- EF67EF14, EF67EF18, EF67EF20 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- integration

- capoeira
- wellness

Project Opportunities:
- Year-end athletics festival combining events, relays, and peer coaching
- Urban adventure challenge in a nearby park with safety briefing
- Capoeira roda demonstration with cultural research component

Assessment Suggestions:
- Athletics festival performance and sportsmanship rubric
- Peer coaching reflection on giving and receiving feedback
- End-of-year wellness portfolio linking nutrition logs, training plans, and personal goals
