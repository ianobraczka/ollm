==================================================

Grade: 5

Subject: Science

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Fifth-grade science at Colégio Horizonte develops scientific literacy through inquiry into living systems, ecological relationships, and human health. Students observe local biodiversity, investigate how organisms depend on one another and their environments, and connect body systems to daily habits such as nutrition, exercise, and hygiene. Fieldwork on the school grounds and nearby green areas anchors abstract concepts in the Brazilian biomes students can directly experience.

Core Competencies:
- Observe, record, and classify living organisms using scientific criteria
- Explain relationships among organisms within ecosystems and food webs
- Describe major human body systems and their roles in maintaining health
- Design fair investigations and communicate findings using evidence

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Living things share fundamental characteristics and can be classified by observable features.

Topics:
- Characteristics of living organisms: nutrition, reproduction, growth, response
- Classification of plants and animals using keys and field guides
- Microscopic life: introduction to microorganisms in soil and water
- Native and introduced species in urban and school environments

Learning Objectives:
- Distinguish living from non-living things using established criteria
- Use classification tools to group local plants and animals
- Describe basic needs shared by all living organisms
- Record observations with labeled diagrams, dates, and conditions
- Discuss how human activity introduces or removes species from an area

BNCC Skills:
- EF05CI01, EF05CI02, EF05CI03

Keywords:
- living things
- classification
- observation
- biodiversity
- investigation
- environment

Project Opportunities:
- Schoolyard species inventory with photo documentation
- Living vs. non-living scavenger hunt with evidence cards
- Native plant identification guide for the school garden

Assessment Suggestions:
- Field journal evaluation with observation quality rubric
- Classification quiz using local species examples
- Short written explanation of why a disputed object is living or non-living

==================================================

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Ecosystems are networks of interdependent organisms and physical factors in constant interaction.

Topics:
- Habitats, niches, and adaptation in Brazilian biomes (Atlantic Forest, Cerrado references)
- Food chains and food webs in local ecosystems
- Producers, consumers, and decomposers
- Energy flow and matter cycling at introductory level

Learning Objectives:
- Define ecosystem, habitat, population, and community with local examples
- Construct food webs showing energy transfer among organisms
- Explain how structural and behavioral adaptations aid survival
- Identify human actions that disrupt or restore ecosystem balance
- Predict consequences of removing one species from a food web

BNCC Skills:
- EF05CI04, EF05CI05, EF05CI06, EF05CI07

Keywords:
- ecosystems
- food webs
- adaptation
- environment
- sustainability
- interdependence

Project Opportunities:
- Ecosystem diorama of a local biome with labeled relationships
- Water consumption study: track classroom usage and link to ecosystem water cycles (math link)
- Poster campaign on protecting a threatened local species

Assessment Suggestions:
- Food web diagram with written explanation of energy flow
- Case study analysis of a local environmental change
- Group presentation on one Brazilian biome and its key species

==================================================

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
The human body is an integrated system whose health depends on balanced nutrition, activity, and care.

Topics:
- Major body systems: skeletal, muscular, digestive, circulatory, respiratory
- Organs and their functions at an introductory level
- Nutrition: food groups, balanced diet, and hydration
- Hygiene, disease prevention, and vaccination awareness

Learning Objectives:
- Name major organs and associate them with primary functions
- Explain how the digestive and circulatory systems work together
- Analyze a daily meal plan for nutritional balance
- Describe hygiene practices that reduce disease transmission
- Connect lifestyle choices to short- and long-term health outcomes

BNCC Skills:
- EF05CI08, EF05CI09, EF05CI10

Keywords:
- human body
- health
- nutrition
- systems
- hygiene
- wellbeing

Project Opportunities:
- Weekly food diary with nutritional analysis (math link for quantities and graphs)
- Health awareness brochure for younger students at the school
- Heart rate investigation before and after exercise with data recording

Assessment Suggestions:
- Body system matching and function explanation test
- Nutrition label analysis worksheet
- Lab report on a simple physiology investigation

==================================================

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Scientific inquiry and environmental stewardship empower students to investigate and improve their communities.

Topics:
- Designing fair tests: variables, controls, and repeated trials
- Data collection, tables, and simple graphing of results
- Water, soil, and air quality indicators on school grounds
- Waste reduction, recycling, and composting in school settings

Learning Objectives:
- Pose investigable questions and identify independent and dependent variables
- Plan and conduct a controlled classroom or schoolyard experiment
- Organize data in tables and interpret trends in graphs
- Propose evidence-based actions to reduce environmental impact at school
- Synthesize how human health depends on healthy ecosystems

BNCC Skills:
- EF05CI01, EF05CI06, EF05CI10 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- investigation
- data
- environment
- sustainability
- health
- collaboration
- problem solving

Project Opportunities:
- School biodiversity survey with tally sheets and bar graphs (math and digital education link)
- Classroom waste audit with reduction plan presentation
- Community sustainability project: compost bin design and implementation

Assessment Suggestions:
- Full inquiry report with hypothesis, method, data, and conclusion
- Environmental action plan evaluated for feasibility and evidence
- End-of-year concept map linking units across the year
