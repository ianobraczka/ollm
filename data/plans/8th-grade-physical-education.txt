Grade: 8

Subject: Physical Education

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Eighth-grade physical education at Colégio Horizonte prepares students for lifelong fitness, sports leadership, and holistic wellness beyond school competition. Students design individualized exercise programs, rotate through player, referee, and coach roles in team sports, and explore body awareness through conditioning and conscious movement practices. Critical discussions address performance pressures, media ideals, and safe participation in community sport. Rio context includes mapping local facilities from praia courts to municipal gyms, beach soccer and volleyball leadership scenarios, and wellness practices suited to tropical urban living.

Core Competencies:
- Design and sustain individualized exercise programs aligned with personal health goals
- Lead, officiate, and coach in team sports demonstrating tactical knowledge and fair judgment
- Evaluate wellness practices including rest, stress management, and body awareness
- Advocate for inclusive, safe access to physical activity in school and community settings

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Lifelong fitness depends on self-directed exercise habits that balance enjoyment, health needs, and realistic goals rather than short-term performance alone.

Topics:
- Principles of program design: frequency, intensity, time, and type (FITT)
- Cardiorespiratory training: running, cycling alternatives, and interval work
- Resistance training with bodyweight and available school equipment
- Flexibility and recovery protocols for sustained participation
- Monitoring progress and adjusting programs based on results and enjoyment

Learning Objectives:
- Create a four-week personal fitness program targeting individual health goals
- Apply FITT principles to structure balanced weekly exercise routines
- Demonstrate safe technique in cardiovascular and resistance exercises
- Track progress using logs, perceived exertion, and simple fitness measures
- Explain how lifelong activity choices differ from elite sport training demands

BNCC Skills:
- EF89EF07, EF89EF08, EF89EF09

Keywords:
- lifelong fitness
- exercise programming
- FITT
- self-directed training
- recovery
- health goals

Project Opportunities:
- Personal four-week fitness program with weekly log and adjustment notes (math link)
- Comparison essay: school PE training versus independent gym or praia workouts in Rio
- Class gallery of diverse lifelong activities students plan to continue after graduation

Assessment Suggestions:
- Fitness program design rubric for safety, balance, and personalization
- Progress journal review with teacher conference on goal adjustment
- Practical demonstration of core exercises with technique feedback

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Sports leadership grows when students understand rules deeply, make fair decisions, and guide others with respect for safety and inclusion.

Topics:
- Player, referee, and coach roles in futsal, volleyball, and handball
- Rule interpretation, signaling, and conflict management during games
- Basic tactical systems: formations, rotations, and substitution strategy
- Event organization: brackets, schedules, and equipment management
- Ethics in sport: doping awareness, corruption, and media representation

Learning Objectives:
- Officiate a game applying rules consistently and communicating decisions clearly
- Plan basic team tactics and communicate strategy during timeouts or breaks
- Organize a small tournament including schedule, roles, and safety checklist
- Analyze how media portrays athletes and discuss effects on youth expectations
- Demonstrate leadership that prioritizes inclusion and participant well-being

BNCC Skills:
- EF89EF01, EF89EF02, EF89EF03, EF89EF05

Keywords:
- sports leadership
- officiating
- coaching
- tactics
- ethics
- tournament organization

Project Opportunities:
- Student-organized round-robin tournament with full officiating rotation
- Tactical playbook for one sport including formations and set plays
- Media analysis project on sports ethics case study with class presentation

Assessment Suggestions:
- Officiating and coaching observation rubric during tournament matches
- Tournament organization checklist completed by student committees
- Written analysis of one ethical issue in contemporary sport

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Wellness integrates physical conditioning with body awareness, stress management, and critical reflection on health messages in society.

Topics:
- Conditioning versus conscious body practices: comparing approaches and benefits
- Yoga, breathing, and mobility routines for stress reduction and focus
- Sleep, academic pressure, and physical activity balance for adolescents
- Body image, social media, and unrealistic performance or beauty standards
- Heat, sun exposure, and safe outdoor activity in Rio's climate

Learning Objectives:
- Compare conditioning fitness and body-awareness practices using BNCC distinctions
- Lead a short mindfulness or mobility session for classmates
- Develop a personal wellness routine addressing stress, sleep, and activity
- Critique media messages that link appearance to athletic worth
- Apply sun safety and hydration strategies for outdoor Rio activities

BNCC Skills:
- EF89EF10, EF89EF11, EF89EF08

Keywords:
- wellness
- body awareness
- stress management
- body image
- mindfulness
- self-care

Project Opportunities:
- Wellness week initiative planned by students for Colégio Horizonte
- Body image awareness campaign using positive, evidence-based messaging
- Personal wellness contract integrating movement, rest, and digital balance

Assessment Suggestions:
- Wellness routine proposal evaluated for feasibility and holistic balance
- Facilitation rubric for leading a body-awareness session
- Critical essay on one media source shaping youth health perceptions

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Students ready for secondary school and adult life advocate for their own wellness and help others access safe, meaningful physical activity in community spaces.

Topics:
- Mapping community sport and recreation resources in Rio neighborhoods
- Adventure and nature activities: hiking safety, environmental respect, and access equity
- World martial arts and cultural respect: technique, history, and stereotype critique
- Capstone integration: fitness programming, leadership, and wellness advocacy
- Transition planning: maintaining active habits through high school and beyond

Learning Objectives:
- Research and present community facilities available for lifelong physical activity
- Plan a safe adventure activity respecting environmental and personal risk factors
- Participate in martial arts practice with cultural appreciation and safety protocols
- Synthesize eighth-grade learning in a wellness and leadership portfolio
- Articulate a realistic plan for staying active after leaving Colégio Horizonte

BNCC Skills:
- EF89EF06, EF89EF16, EF89EF19 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- community access
- advocacy
- adventure
- integration
- transition
- lifelong wellness

Project Opportunities:
- Community sport resource guide for families near Colégio Horizonte
- Adventure activity proposal with risk assessment and environmental guidelines
- Graduation wellness portfolio presented in student-led conference

Assessment Suggestions:
- Community resource project evaluated for accuracy and practical usefulness
- Adventure safety plan rubric with peer and teacher review
- Capstone portfolio and conference covering fitness, leadership, and wellness goals
