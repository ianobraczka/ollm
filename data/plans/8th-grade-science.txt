==================================================

Grade: 8

Subject: Science

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Eighth-grade science at Colégio Horizonte integrates physics and chemistry foundations with sustainability science to prepare students for high school laboratory work and informed citizenship. Students investigate forces, motion, energy, and waves alongside atomic structure, chemical reactions, and properties of materials. Sustainability serves as a culminating lens, requiring students to apply scientific principles to resource use, climate resilience, and green technology. Quantitative methods from mathematics and data tools from digital education are embedded in all units.

Core Competencies:
- Apply principles of mechanics and energy to explain motion and physical interactions
- Describe atomic and molecular structure and predict products of simple chemical reactions
- Evaluate sustainability challenges using scientific evidence and systems thinking
- Design and conduct investigations with controlled variables, accurate measurement, and ethical reporting

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Physics foundations explain motion and forces through measurable quantities and conservation laws.

Topics:
- Position, displacement, speed, velocity, and acceleration
- Newton's laws of motion in everyday and laboratory contexts
- Forces: gravity, friction, normal force, and free-body diagrams
- Work, energy, and power; kinetic and potential energy conversions
- Simple machines: mechanical advantage in school and community applications

Learning Objectives:
- Distinguish speed and velocity; calculate average speed from data
- Draw and interpret free-body diagrams for objects at rest and in motion
- Apply Newton's laws to predict effects of forces on motion
- Track energy transformations in a closed system demonstration
- Calculate mechanical advantage for levers and inclined planes used in design

BNCC Skills:
- EF08CI01, EF08CI02, EF08CI03, EF08CI04

Keywords:
- physics
- forces
- energy
- motion
- investigation
- problem solving

Project Opportunities:
- Motion graphing lab: distance-time and velocity-time graphs (math link)
- Simple machine design challenge for a school accessibility improvement
- Roller coaster energy tracker using height and speed measurements

Assessment Suggestions:
- Free-body diagram quiz with written explanations
- Motion data lab report with graphs and calculated speeds
- Energy transformation diagram for a multi-step real system

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Chemistry foundations describe matter at the atomic scale and predict how substances interact and transform.

Topics:
- Atomic structure: protons, neutrons, electrons; periodic table organization
- Chemical bonds: ionic and covalent (introductory models)
- Chemical equations and conservation of atoms in reactions
- Acids, bases, and pH in household and environmental contexts
- Reaction rates and factors: temperature, concentration, surface area

Learning Objectives:
- Use the periodic table to identify element properties and group patterns
- Build molecular models representing simple compounds
- Balance chemical equations for introductory synthesis and decomposition reactions
- Measure and interpret pH in water samples from school sources
- Design a fair test examining one factor affecting reaction rate

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Waves and electromagnetic radiation connect physics to communication technology and environmental monitoring.

Topics:
- Wave properties: amplitude, wavelength, frequency, and speed
- Sound waves and hearing; noise pollution measurement
- Light and the electromagnetic spectrum
- Reflection, refraction, and color; lenses and mirrors (introductory)
- Applications: fiber optics, satellite data, solar radiation, and remote sensing

Learning Objectives:
- Describe wave characteristics using correct vocabulary and units
- Explain how sound travels through media and measure noise levels
- Map the electromagnetic spectrum with everyday source examples
- Predict reflection and refraction behavior using ray diagrams
- Connect electromagnetic technologies to environmental data collection

Project Opportunities:

- Solar panel efficiency investigation with radiation and output data

- Wave parameter calculation worksheet
- Ray diagram task for reflection and refraction cases
- Technology application brief linking waves to a sustainability tool

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Sustainability science integrates physics, chemistry, and ecology to guide responsible innovation for future generations.

Topics:
- Life cycle assessment of products and services
- Circular economy principles: reduce, reuse, recycle, redesign
- Renewable energy systems: solar, wind, hydro, and biomass in Brazil
- Carbon footprint calculation and offset concepts
- Green chemistry and clean technology introductions
- Capstone: school sustainability plan backed by scientific evidence

Learning Objectives:
- Trace materials and energy through a product life cycle diagram
- Compare environmental trade-offs of energy sources using data
- Calculate a simplified carbon footprint for a school activity
- Propose a sustainability improvement with predicted scientific impact
- Communicate findings integrating physics, chemistry, and systems thinking

BNCC Skills:
- EF08CI12, EF08CI13, EF08CI14 (integration and application)

Keywords:
- sustainability
- innovation
- environment
- energy
- ethics
- data
- collaboration

Project Opportunities:
- Community sustainability project with measured outcomes and public presentation
- School energy audit recommending upgrades with cost-benefit analysis (math link)
- Interdisciplinary capstone: data collection, linear modeling, digital dashboard, and science report

Assessment Suggestions:
- Life cycle assessment poster with data-supported claims
- Energy source comparison essay with cited Brazilian statistics
- Capstone rubric evaluating scientific accuracy, data use, and action feasibility
