Grade: 8

Subject: Visual Arts

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Eighth-grade visual arts at Colégio Horizonte culminates the fundamental years with portfolio development, ambitious mixed-media projects, and a capstone exhibition. Students consolidate drawing, painting, design, sculpture, printmaking, and digital skills into a coherent body of work that reflects personal voice and cultural awareness. Exhibition planning teaches curation, installation, and audience engagement while critical writing and presentation build advocacy for the arts. Brazilian artistic heritage—from folk traditions through Portinari and Tarsila to contemporary voices—frames students' capstone themes as they prepare for high school art pathways.

Core Competencies:
- Assemble and present a professional-quality portfolio demonstrating range, growth, and intent
- Execute mixed-media projects that integrate techniques across two- and three-dimensional forms
- Plan, install, and document a capstone exhibition for the school community
- Articulate artistic choices through artist statements, critiques, and public presentation

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
A strong portfolio selects, organizes, and presents artwork to communicate artistic identity and development over time.

Topics:
- Portfolio types: physical, digital, and college or program application awareness
- Selecting representative works: strongest pieces, range of media, and process evidence
- Artist statement drafting: theme, influences, techniques, and goals
- Photography and scanning standards for accurate color and scale reproduction
- Peer portfolio reviews using structured feedback protocols

Learning Objectives:
- Curate ten to fifteen works that show technical range and conceptual coherence
- Write a concise artist statement revisable across the school year
- Photograph or scan artwork with consistent lighting, cropping, and resolution
- Organize a digital portfolio folder with filenames, dates, and media labels
- Give and receive portfolio feedback that identifies strengths and specific improvements

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR06, EF69AR07, EF69AR08, EF69AR35

Keywords:
- portfolio
- curation
- artist statement
- documentation
- presentation
- reflection

Project Opportunities:
- Digital portfolio build in structured folders with artist statement v1
- Portfolio cover and title page design reflecting personal visual identity
- Mock portfolio review with guest teacher or upper-grade student panel

Assessment Suggestions:
- Portfolio rubric for selection quality, documentation, and statement clarity
- Image quality checklist applied to all portfolio photographs
- Peer review form with required evidence-based comments

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Mixed-media practice combines materials, processes, and ideas that single-medium work cannot express alone.

Topics:
- Mixed-media strategies: collage, assemblage, layering, and media juxtaposition
- Combining drawing, painting, print, digital elements, and sculptural relief
- Material experimentation with intentional limits: sustainability and studio safety
- Brazilian mixed-media references: contemporary assemblage, neo-concrete collage echoes
- Concept development: mood boards, material tests, and iterative prototypes

Learning Objectives:
- Plan a mixed-media work with tested material combinations before final execution
- Integrate at least three distinct techniques in one cohesive composition or object
- Justify material choices in relation to theme, texture, and symbolic meaning
- Resolve formal problems such as adhesion, surface compatibility, and structural support
- Document experiments that failed or were revised, showing process integrity

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR05, EF69AR06, EF69AR07, EF69AR32

Keywords:
- mixed media
- assemblage
- collage
- experimentation
- materials
- concept

Project Opportunities:
- Large mixed-media panel on a personal or community theme with process journal
- Assemblage sculpture combining found objects, wire, and painted surfaces
- Material test grid documenting compatible combinations for capstone planning

Assessment Suggestions:
- Mixed-media project rubric for integration, craftsmanship, and concept
- Process journal evaluation with dated experiments and revision notes
- Mid-project critique focusing on whether media choices support the theme

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Capstone artworks express sustained inquiry into a theme connecting personal voice, technique, and Brazilian cultural context.

Topics:
- Capstone theme selection: identity, environment, social justice, memory, or community
- Sustained project timelines: milestones, critiques, and revision cycles
- Advanced integration of drawing, painting, sculpture, print, or digital methods
- Brazilian art influences in capstone work: folk, modern, and contemporary references
- Legal and ethical considerations for imagery, representation, and public display

Learning Objectives:
- Maintain a capstone timeline with completed milestones and documented critiques
- Produce a finished capstone artwork or small related series at exhibition quality
- Reference at least one Brazilian artistic tradition or artist meaningfully in the work or statement
- Revise capstone work substantively after at least two rounds of structured feedback
- Prepare display-ready presentation including title, labels, and installation notes

- EF69AR01, EF69AR06, EF69AR31, EF69AR33, EF69AR34

Keywords:
- capstone
- theme
- revision
- Brazilian culture
- inquiry
- exhibition

Project Opportunities:
- Capstone artwork with annotated research board linking theme to Brazilian visual references
- Cross-curricular capstone option connecting visual arts to social studies or science topic
- Studio work sessions with rotating critique groups and revision logs

Assessment Suggestions:
- Capstone milestone checklist and timeline adherence review
- Rubric-scored final artwork for concept, execution, and cultural connection
- Revision log demonstrating response to critique with specific changes identified

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
A capstone exhibition shares artistic learning with the community and marks the transition to advanced study.

Topics:
- Exhibition planning: theme, venue, layout, lighting, and audience experience
- Roles in exhibition production: curator, installer, docent, designer, and publicist
- Opening event preparation: invitations, program, and artist talks
- Post-exhibition reflection and portfolio update with capstone documentation
- Transition planning: high school art options, continued practice, and community resources

Learning Objectives:
- Collaborate to install a grade-level exhibition with professional presentation standards
- Write exhibition labels and a curatorial statement connecting works to shared themes
- Present artwork orally to visitors using clear, evidence-based language
- Update portfolio and artist statement to include capstone work and exhibition photos
- Identify next-step opportunities for visual arts study beyond eighth grade

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR08, EF69AR31, EF69AR34, EF69AR35 (integration and culmination)

Keywords:
- exhibition
- curation
- installation
- presentation
- community
- culmination

Project Opportunities:
- Grade 8 capstone exhibition in school gallery, hallway, or multipurpose space
- Artist talk or guided tour for families, staff, and younger students
- Exhibition catalog or digital slideshow with curatorial statement and artist pages

Assessment Suggestions:
- Exhibition participation rubric covering installation, collaboration, and punctuality
- Oral presentation evaluation during opening event or class critique
- Final portfolio and artist statement v2 with capstone and exhibition documentation
- End-of-year synthesis reflection on four years of visual arts at Colégio Horizonte
