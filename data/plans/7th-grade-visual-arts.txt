Grade: 7

Subject: Visual Arts

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Seventh-grade visual arts at Colégio Horizonte expands students' range through digital art tools, sustained sculpture practice, and structured art criticism. Learners investigate how technology extends traditional studio methods while grounding digital work in the same elements and principles mastered in earlier years. Three-dimensional projects develop spatial reasoning and material problem-solving, and critical writing strengthens the ability to interpret artworks in cultural and historical context. Brazilian artistic heritage—including references to Portinari's social realism and Tarsila's modernist forms—supports comparative analysis across media and eras.

Core Competencies:
- Create and refine digital artworks using responsible technology and design principles
- Construct sculptural works that demonstrate structural planning, material choice, and surface treatment
- Write and present art criticism using evidence-based description, analysis, and interpretation
- Connect visual art practices to social, cultural, and ethical dimensions of contemporary life

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Digital tools extend visual art practice when artists apply design principles and ethical judgment to new media.

Topics:
- Introduction to digital art: raster vs. vector concepts and appropriate software workflows
- Digital drawing tablets, layers, and non-destructive editing basics
- Color, composition, and typography in screen-based design
- Ethics of image use: copyright, attribution, AI awareness, and personal data

Learning Objectives:
- Navigate basic digital art software to create an original layered composition
- Apply elements and principles of design deliberately in a digital format
- Export files in appropriate resolutions for print and screen display
- Explain ethical responsibilities when sourcing references and sharing work online
- Compare advantages and limits of digital versus traditional media for specific goals

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR03, EF69AR05, EF69AR35

Keywords:
- digital art
- layers
- design
- ethics
- technology
- composition

Project Opportunities:
- Digital poster redesign for a school event using original drawings and typography
- Hybrid illustration: ink drawing scanned and colored digitally
- Class discussion and infographic on responsible image sharing (digital education link)

Assessment Suggestions:
- Digital artwork rubric for composition, technique, and file management
- Short written policy on ethical image use in student projects
- Before-and-after comparison of hand-drawn and digitally enhanced versions

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Sculpture engages space, structure, and material properties to transform ideas into tangible three-dimensional form.

Topics:
- Sculpture planning: armatures, maquettes, and scale models
- Clay modeling, plaster gauze, wire, and mixed-material construction
- Subtractive carving introduction with soft materials and safe tool protocols
- Environmental and installation sculpture concepts for school or community contexts

Learning Objectives:
- Build an armature or internal support appropriate to the chosen sculpture scale
- Demonstrate additive and subtractive methods in at least two different materials
- Solve structural problems such as balance, weight distribution, and joint reinforcement
- Plan a sculpture from sketches through maquette to finished piece with revisions
- Analyze how scale and placement change viewer experience of a sculptural work

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR04, EF69AR05, EF69AR06

Keywords:
- sculpture
- form
- armature
- materials
- structure
- installation

Project Opportunities:
- Figurative or abstract clay sculpture with armature and expressive surface
- Wire and mixed-material mobile or stabile exploring balance and movement
- Small installation proposal for a school space with measured sketch and material list

Assessment Suggestions:
- Sculpture rubric evaluating structure, craftsmanship, concept, and finish
- Process documentation with photos of maquette, armature, and final work
- Peer feedback session using sculpture-specific critique prompts

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Art criticism develops informed viewers who support interpretations with close looking and contextual knowledge.

Topics:
- Feldman model adaptation: description, analysis, interpretation, and judgment
- Visual analysis of line, form, color, composition, and material choices
- Contextual criticism: artist biography, historical period, and cultural setting
- Brazilian art case studies: Portinari's social themes and Tarsila's modernist vision

Learning Objectives:
- Write a structured critique of an artwork using the four-step critical model
- Support interpretive claims with specific references to visible evidence
- Place a Brazilian artwork in its historical and cultural context accurately
- Compare critical responses to the same work and explain differences respectfully
- Revise a critique after peer or teacher feedback to strengthen reasoning

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR01, EF69AR02, EF69AR07, EF69AR33

Keywords:
- art criticism
- analysis
- interpretation
- context
- evidence
- Brazilian art

Project Opportunities:
- Formal critique essay on a museum or online collection work with citation
- Classroom seminar presenting opposing interpretations of one shared artwork
- Comparative study chart: Portinari and Tarsila on theme, style, and historical moment

Assessment Suggestions:
- Rubric-scored critical essay for description accuracy and interpretive depth
- Oral critique demonstrating live analysis of a projected artwork
- Revision log showing how feedback improved the final written critique

==================================================

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Synthesizing digital, sculptural, and critical skills prepares students to pursue independent visual inquiry in eighth grade.

Topics:
- Cross-media capstone preparation: proposal, timeline, and material or software list
- Documentation photography and basic portfolio layout for mixed media bodies of work
- Art careers and cultural production: artist, curator, designer, educator, and conservator paths
- Collaborative critique circles with structured roles: facilitator, note-taker, and responder

Learning Objectives:
- Draft a feasible capstone proposal with theme, media, and assessment criteria
- Document three-dimensional and digital work with consistent lighting and labeling
- Identify professional pathways in the Brazilian visual arts ecosystem
- Lead or contribute to a structured peer critique using evidence-based language
- Reflect on technical and conceptual growth across all four quarters

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR06, EF69AR08, EF69AR31, EF69AR34 (integration)

Keywords:
- synthesis
- proposal
- documentation
- careers
- critique
- portfolio

Project Opportunities:
- Capstone proposal with mood board, timeline, and resource plan for eighth grade
- Portfolio photo session for best sculpture and digital pieces of the year
- Career research panel: interview or profile a local artist, designer, or curator

Assessment Suggestions:
- Capstone proposal rubric for clarity, feasibility, and creative ambition
- Portfolio documentation checklist with image quality standards
- Comprehensive reflection essay linking units to personal artistic development
