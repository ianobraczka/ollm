==================================================

Grade: 7

Subject: Brazilian Social Studies

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Seventh-grade Brazilian Social Studies at Colégio Horizonte traces Brazil through the twentieth century, from the Old Republic's oligarchic politics to Getúlio Vargas's transformative governments and the restoration of democratic institutions. Students study urbanization, industrialization, labor movements, and the authoritarian turn of the Estado Novo. Rio de Janeiro's prominence as a political and cultural capital helps students connect national narratives to architecture, media, and public life they can still observe.

Core Competencies:
- Analyze political and economic shifts that modernized twentieth-century Brazil
- Evaluate the Vargas era's reforms, propaganda, and authoritarian practices
- Explain how labor, migration, and urban growth reshaped Brazilian society
- Compare democratic and dictatorial governance using historical evidence

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
The early twentieth-century republic expanded industry and cities while concentrating power among regional elites.

Topics:
- Old Republic (1889–1930): coronelismo, café com leite, and limited suffrage
- Immigration, railroads, and factory growth in São Paulo and Rio
- The 1922 Modern Art Week and new cultural identities
- Tenentismo, Prestes Column, and challenges to oligarchic rule

Learning Objectives:
- Characterize political structures of the Old Republic with regional examples
- Explain how immigration and industry transformed urban landscapes
- Describe cultural movements that questioned elite-dominated narratives
- Identify military and civilian protests that weakened oligarchic control
- Use graphs and maps to show early twentieth-century urban growth

BNCC Skills:
- EF07HI02, EF07HI05, EF07HI07

Keywords:
- Old Republic
- urbanization
- industry
- oligarchy
- migration
- modernism

Project Opportunities:
- Data poster on Rio and São Paulo population growth 1900–1930
- Modernism gallery with student interpretations of 1922 artistic manifestos
- Newspaper front page covering the 1930 Revolution from multiple viewpoints

Assessment Suggestions:
- Chart interpretation on industrial and urban statistics
- Short answer test on Old Republic political vocabulary
- Primary source analysis of a tenentista manifesto or photograph

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Getúlio Vargas led Brazil through crises and reforms that expanded labor rights while centralizing state power.

Topics:
- 1930 Revolution and provisional government consolidation
- Estado Novo (1937–1945): propaganda, censorship, and centralization
- Labor legislation: CLT, minimum wage, and social security foundations
- Vargas in Rio: public works, radio, and construction of national symbols

Learning Objectives:
- Explain how Vargas rose to power and reshaped federal authority
- Describe major labor laws and their effects on workers' lives
- Analyze propaganda techniques used by the Estado Novo government
- Evaluate benefits and costs of Vargas's authoritarian modernization
- Compare Vargas-era reforms with earlier republican policies

BNCC Skills:
- EF07HI06, EF07HI09, EF07HI10

Keywords:
- Vargas
- Estado Novo
- labor rights
- propaganda
- CLT
- modernization

Project Opportunities:
- CLT rights brochure explaining worker protections in plain language
- Analysis of Estado Novo posters, radio scripts, or architectural projects in Rio
- Debate on whether Vargas should be remembered primarily as reformer or dictator

Assessment Suggestions:
- Policy comparison table: Old Republic versus Vargas labor laws
- Document-based questions on Estado Novo propaganda images
- Essay outlining three lasting effects of the Vargas era

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Postwar Brazil pursued development and democracy before a military coup suspended civil liberties for two decades.

Topics:
- Second Republic (1945–1964): parties, elections, and developmentalism
- JK's Brasília project and automobile industry growth
- Rural migration, favelization, and urban inequality in Rio and other cities
- 1964 military coup: causes, Cold War context, and initial reforms

Learning Objectives:
- Describe democratic institutions restored after Vargas and World War II
- Explain how developmentalist policies changed Brazil's economic geography
- Analyze social effects of rapid urbanization on housing and inequality
- Identify factors that led to the 1964 military takeover
- Recognize forms of resistance including arts, student movements, and unions

BNCC Skills:
- EF07HI17, EF07HI18, EF07HI19, EF07HI20

Keywords:
- democracy
- developmentalism
- dictatorship
- urban inequality
- resistance
- Cold War

Project Opportunities:
- Brasília design exhibit connecting politics, architecture, and national identity
- Oral history-style project on neighborhood change in a Rio community
- Timeline of dictatorship events with symbols for repression and resistance

Assessment Suggestions:
- Cause-and-effect organizer for the 1964 coup
- Map activity linking industrial centers and new capital construction
- Written response using a resistance song or poem as evidence

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Democratic transitions require defending rights won through struggle and embedding them in law and daily practice.

Topics:
- Diretas Já movement and gradual political opening in the 1980s
- Constituent Assembly and the 1988 Constitution's civil and social rights
- Truth, memory, and accountability for dictatorship-era abuses
- Afro-Brazilian, indigenous, and women's movements in democratic Brazil

Learning Objectives:
- Explain how social movements pressured the return to civilian rule
- Identify key rights guaranteed by the 1988 Constitution
- Discuss why societies debate memory and justice after dictatorship
- Relate historical exclusion to contemporary inclusion policies
- Synthesize how economy, politics, and culture interacted across the century

BNCC Skills:
- EF07HI21, EF07HI22, EF07HI23, EF07HI09

Keywords:
- constitution
- democracy
- human rights
- social movements
- memory
- citizenship

Project Opportunities:
- Constitutional rights charter illustrated for younger students at school
- Mock truth commission hearing based on documented dictatorship cases
- Capstone documentary storyboard on Rio's twentieth-century transformations

Assessment Suggestions:
- 1988 Constitution scavenger hunt matching articles to daily life scenarios
- End-of-year chronology spanning 1930–1988 with explanatory captions
- Reflective essay connecting Vargas reforms, dictatorship, and democratic recovery
