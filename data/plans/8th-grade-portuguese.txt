==================================================

Grade: 8

Subject: Portuguese

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Eighth-grade Portuguese at Colégio Horizonte prepares students for secondary education and high-stakes writing by engaging canonical Brazilian literature, advanced argumentation, and ENEM-style textual production. Students analyze classic and contemporary works, produce opinion articles and dissertations with structural rigor, and refine syntax and cohesion for formal registers. Critical media reading and extended research mirror both BNCC Anos Finais expectations and US approaches to rhetorical analysis and document-based writing.

Core Competencies:
- Interpret canonical and contemporary literary texts with attention to form, context, and intertextuality
- Produce argumentative dissertations and opinion articles with thesis, development, and conclusion
- Apply advanced grammar and cohesion resources in formal written production
- Evaluate information sources and synthesize research for academic and civic purposes

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Canonical literature reflects historical contexts and aesthetic traditions, and mature readers interpret works through structure, style, and cultural dialogue.

Topics:
- Brazilian literary canon: Machado de Assis, Lima Barreto, and modern short fiction
- Lyric chronicle and short forms: voice, irony, and social observation
- Drama and film adaptation: comparing script, performance, and directorial choices
- Intertextuality and parody in poetry and contemporary reworkings

Learning Objectives:
- Read canonical texts independently applying strategies suited to complex language
- Analyze dramatic organization in theater, television, or film versions of literary works
- Identify intertextual references and parodic reworkings in poems and short texts
- Explain effects of irony, metaphor, and antithesis in selected literary passages
- Draft a short literary analysis essay with clear thesis and textual evidence

BNCC Skills:
- EF89LP33, EF89LP34, EF89LP35, EF89LP37, EF08LP09

Keywords:
- canonical literature
- literary essay
- intertextuality
- Machado de Assis
- drama
- close reading

Project Opportunities:
- Adapted scene performance from a Machado short story with director's analytical preface
- Parody poetry project reworking a classic sonnet or cordel with author reflection
- Comparative study of a Rio-set chronicle and its film or theater adaptation

Assessment Suggestions:
- Literary analysis essay rubric aligned to ENEM competência de interpretação
- Oral explication of one passage linking device to thematic effect
- Canonical authors matching quiz with brief justification of each work's significance

==================================================

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
ENEM-style and academic argumentation require explicit thesis, logical development, counter-argument, and linguistic resources that signal conviction and nuance.

Topics:
- Dissertação argumentativa: introduction, development paragraphs, and conclusion
- Artigo de opinião: audience, editorial context, and persuasive structure
- Explicit and implicit arguments; concession and refutation
- Modalization in argument: adverbs, verbs, punctuation, and evaluative lexicon

Learning Objectives:
- Produce an opinion article defending a position with arguments and counter-arguments
- Identify explicit and implicit theses and evaluate strength of reasoning in model texts
- Use cohesive sequencers and referential ties in multi-paragraph argumentative writing
- Apply modal and argumentative resources to calibrate tone and persuasiveness
- Revise drafts using a checklist based on ENEM writing competences

BNCC Skills:
- EF08LP03, EF89LP04, EF08LP16, EF89LP10, EF08LP13

Keywords:
- ENEM writing
- dissertation
- opinion article
- argumentation
- counter-argument
- modalization

Project Opportunities:
- Timed ENEM-style writing practice on a contemporary social theme with peer scoring
- Editorial board producing a booklet of student opinion articles on school and city issues
- Argument repair clinic: strengthen weak sample essays using counter-argument and evidence

Assessment Suggestions:
- ENEM-aligned rubric scoring mastery, organization, cohesion, and register
- Outline and plan evaluation before full essay production
- Comparative analysis of two opinion pieces on the same controversy

==================================================

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Mastery of syntax and cohesion at the sentence and period level enables precise, sophisticated writing expected in secondary school and standardized assessments.

Topics:
- Clause constituents: subject modifiers, verbal complements, and adjuncts
- Active and passive voice; effects on agency and formality
- Coordination and subordination; frequent subordinate conjunctions
- Sequential cohesion: articulators, conjunctions, and paragraph transitions

Learning Objectives:
- Analyze clause constituents in complex sentences from literary and essay texts
- Interpret effects of passive constructions and select voice appropriately in writing
- Identify coordinated and subordinated periods and incorporate subordination in essays
- Use sequential cohesive devices to strengthen logical flow between paragraphs
- Edit essays for regency, agreement, and punctuation in formal contexts

BNCC Skills:
- EF08LP06, EF08LP08, EF08LP11, EF08LP12, EF08LP14

Keywords:
- syntax
- subordination
- passive voice
- cohesion
- formal register
- editing

Project Opportunities:
- Sentence combining and expansion drills applied to ENEM essay body paragraphs
- Peer editing teams focused on cohesion markers and subordinate clause variety
- Grammar in literature: collect syntactic patterns from a canonical text and explain effects

Assessment Suggestions:
- Syntax and cohesion diagnostic using sentences from student essays
- Edited essay comparison showing measurable improvement in formal features
- Short test on voice, subordination, and articulator use in context

==================================================

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Independent research, critical journalism, and synthetic writing demonstrate readiness for secondary school by integrating reading, inquiry, and public communication.

Topics:
- Extended research: open sources, surveys, and synthesis across disciplines
- Resenha and analytical review genres with structured evaluation criteria
- Journalism literacy: editorials, fact-checking, and digital news ecosystems
- Progression and thematic threading in long expository and argumentative texts

Learning Objectives:
- Conduct extended research with documented sources and ethical use of information
- Produce a resenha or critical review organizing description and evaluative judgment
- Compare news editorials and verify claims using fact-checking strategies
- Maintain thematic progression across a multi-paragraph text using retakes and links
- Present a capstone portfolio demonstrating growth across literary, argumentative, and research writing

BNCC Skills:
- EF08LP01, EF08LP02, EF89LP24, EF89LP26, EF89LP29

Keywords:
- research synthesis
- resenha
- fact-checking
- progression
- capstone
- media literacy

Project Opportunities:
- Capstone research and opinion project on a Rio public policy or environmental issue
- Fact-checking challenge using Brazilian verification services on viral claims
- Graduation portfolio presentation with literary analysis, ENEM essay, and research resenha

Assessment Suggestions:
- Capstone rubric integrating inquiry, argument, literary reading, and formal writing
- Simulated ENEM combined interpretive and production section under timed conditions
- Portfolio reflection essay on readiness for ninth grade and continued language study
