==================================================

Grade: 6

Subject: Science

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Sixth-grade science at Colégio Horizonte deepens understanding of life's organization from cells to ecosystems and connects biological diversity to Brazilian biomes and conservation challenges. Students explore Earth systems including the atmosphere, hydrosphere, and geosphere, examining how matter and energy cycle through environments they can observe locally. Laboratory work with microscopes, field sampling, and data recording prepares students for interdisciplinary projects with mathematics and digital education.

Core Competencies:
- Describe cell structure and explain the cell as the basic unit of life
- Analyze biodiversity patterns and human threats to species survival
- Explain interactions among Earth systems and their influence on life
- Conduct investigations using appropriate tools, measurement, and safety protocols

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Cells are the fundamental units of structure and function in all living organisms.

Topics:
- Cell theory and history of microscopy
- Plant vs. animal cell structures and organelle functions
- Unicellular and multicellular organisms
- Microscope technique and prepared slide observation

Learning Objectives:
- State the cell theory and its significance for biology
- Label major organelles and describe their roles
- Compare plant and animal cells using observed specimens
- Use a microscope safely to view cells at appropriate magnification
- Explain how cell specialization supports tissue and organ function

BNCC Skills:
- EF06CI01, EF06CI02, EF06CI03

Keywords:
- cells
- microscopy
- living things
- investigation
- structure
- health

Project Opportunities:
- Cell model construction with labeled organelles and function cards
- Microscopy photo journal of classroom specimens
- Comparison poster: single-celled protists vs. multicellular organisms

Assessment Suggestions:
- Lab practical on microscope setup and focusing
- Cell diagram labeling with written function descriptions
- Short essay linking cell specialization to an organ system

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Biodiversity reflects millions of years of evolution and is essential for resilient ecosystems.

Topics:
- Species, genus, and classification hierarchy
- Brazilian biodiversity hotspots and endemic species
- Threats: habitat loss, invasive species, climate change, poaching
- Conservation strategies: protected areas, breeding programs, legislation

Learning Objectives:
- Define biodiversity at genetic, species, and ecosystem levels
- Identify major threats to biodiversity in Brazil with local examples
- Explain how extinction of one species affects ecosystem stability
- Describe conservation actions taken by communities and government
- Contribute observations to a class or school biodiversity database

BNCC Skills:
- EF06CI04, EF06CI05, EF06CI06, EF06CI07

Keywords:
- biodiversity
- ecosystems
- environment
- sustainability
- conservation
- data
- collaboration

Project Opportunities:
- School biodiversity survey with species tally and habitat notes (math and digital education link)
- Endangered species advocacy presentation with conservation plan
- Invasive species case study on a Brazilian context

Assessment Suggestions:
- Biodiversity concept map with threats and responses
- Field survey rubric evaluating observation quality and identification
- Written argument for protecting one local habitat

==================================================

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Earth systems interact continuously, shaping climates, landscapes, and the conditions for life.

Topics:
- Lithosphere, hydrosphere, atmosphere, and biosphere interactions
- Rock cycle and soil formation (introductory)
- Water cycle: evaporation, condensation, precipitation, collection
- Weather vs. climate; seasons and latitude effects

Learning Objectives:
- Describe the four major Earth systems and give interaction examples
- Explain the water cycle using diagrams and local weather data
- Distinguish weather events from long-term climate patterns
- Classify resources as renewable or non-renewable with Brazilian examples
- Analyze how human extraction affects Earth system balance

BNCC Skills:
- EF06CI08, EF06CI09, EF06CI10, EF06CI11

Keywords:
- Earth systems
- water cycle
- climate
- environment
- resources
- sustainability

Project Opportunities:
- Water consumption study: measure and graph daily school usage (math link)
- Weather data log with weekly graphs and pattern analysis (math link)
- Resource audit: trace origins of classroom materials and environmental cost

Assessment Suggestions:
- Earth systems diagram with labeled interactions
- Water cycle model demonstration or illustrated poster
- Data interpretation quiz using real climate graphs

==================================================

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Integrated scientific thinking connects cellular life, biodiversity, and Earth systems to human responsibility.

Topics:
- Review and synthesis across units
- Environmental impact assessment on school grounds
- Designing solutions: reduce, reuse, recycle, restore
- Science communication: posters, reports, and presentations

Learning Objectives:
- Explain how healthy cells, diverse species, and stable Earth systems depend on one another
- Conduct an environmental impact survey of a school area
- Propose evidence-based improvements with measurable goals
- Communicate scientific findings to peers and community members
- Evaluate sources of scientific information for reliability

BNCC Skills:
- EF06CI06, EF06CI10, EF06CI11 (integration and application)

Keywords:
- environmental impact
- sustainability
- investigation
- data
- communication
- ethics
- innovation

Project Opportunities:
- Community sustainability project with action plan and timeline
- Digital awareness campaign on conservation using student-created media (digital education link)
- Interdisciplinary capstone: biodiversity data collection, statistical analysis, and digital visualization

Assessment Suggestions:
- Environmental impact report with data tables and recommendations
- Oral presentation with visual aids evaluated for scientific accuracy
- End-of-year comprehensive test with scenario-based items
