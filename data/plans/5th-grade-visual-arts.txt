Grade: 5

Subject: Visual Arts

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Fifth-grade visual arts at Colégio Horizonte introduces students to the building blocks of image-making through drawing, painting, design, and introductory sculpture. Students explore the elements and principles of art while connecting classroom work to Brazilian folk traditions, regional crafts, and national heritage. Hands-on experimentation with sustainable materials, collaborative studio routines, and guided appreciation of artworks—including light references to Candido Portinari and Tarsila do Amaral—build visual literacy and creative confidence before middle school specializations.

Core Competencies:
- Identify and apply the elements and principles of visual art in original compositions
- Experiment with drawing, painting, collage, and simple sculptural techniques using varied materials
- Recognize Brazilian folk and traditional art forms as expressions of local and national culture
- Discuss personal and peer artworks with respect, using basic art vocabulary

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Visual artworks are built from fundamental elements that artists combine to create meaning and emotion.

Topics:
- Elements of art: line, shape, form, color, value, texture, and space
- Principles of design: balance, contrast, emphasis, pattern, rhythm, unity, and proportion
- Observational drawing from still life and school environment
- Color theory: primary, secondary, warm and cool palettes, and emotional associations
- Line quality and mark-making with pencil, charcoal, ink, and pastel

Learning Objectives:
- Name and demonstrate each element of art in guided exercises
- Apply at least three principles of design in a finished composition
- Draw objects from observation using proportion and shading techniques
- Mix and apply colors intentionally to support a chosen mood or theme
- Describe how elements and principles work together in a peer artwork

BNCC Skills:
- EF15AR02, EF15AR04, EF15AR06

Keywords:
- elements of art
- principles of design
- drawing
- color
- composition
- observation

Project Opportunities:
- Elements and principles sketchbook with labeled studies
- Still-life drawing series progressing from line to shaded form
- Color wheel collage using recycled paper and magazine clippings

Assessment Suggestions:
- Labeled diagram quiz identifying elements in sample artworks
- Rubric-based evaluation of observational drawing accuracy and composition
- Partner critique using sentence stems and art vocabulary cards

==================================================

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Painting and design transform ideas into visual messages through color, shape, and intentional arrangement.

Topics:
- Painting media: watercolor, tempera, and acrylic introduction with brush control
- Wet-on-wet, layering, and resist techniques for expressive effects
- Introduction to graphic design: layout, typography awareness, and visual hierarchy
- Poster and sign design for school events or community messages
- Safe studio habits: material care, drying racks, and shared workspace routines

Learning Objectives:
- Control brushwork and paint consistency for intended textures and edges
- Plan a painting with thumbnail sketches and a chosen color scheme
- Arrange text and images in a simple poster with clear visual hierarchy
- Explain how design choices guide the viewer's attention
- Reflect on material choices and sustainability in art-making

BNCC Skills:
- EF15AR01, EF15AR04, EF15AR05

Keywords:
- painting
- design
- color
- layout
- expression
- studio practice

Project Opportunities:
- Themed watercolor landscape inspired by a Brazilian biome
- Classroom poster design for a school sustainability campaign (cross-curricular link)
- Mini design challenge: redesign a familiar object label or book cover

Assessment Suggestions:
- Painting technique demonstration with written artist statement
- Design rubric evaluating clarity, balance, and audience awareness
- Studio citizenship checklist and material responsibility reflection

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Brazilian folk art and craft traditions reveal how communities preserve identity, stories, and beauty through visual forms.

Topics:
- Folk art and popular art: cordel literature illustrations, ex-votos, and regional crafts
- Indigenous and Afro-Brazilian visual traditions in contemporary classroom context
- Light study of Candido Portinari and Tarsila do Amaral as national artistic references
- Collage, print stamping, and mixed paper techniques inspired by folk motifs
- Patrimônio cultural: material and immaterial heritage in local and national art

Learning Objectives:
- Identify characteristics of at least two Brazilian folk or traditional art forms
- Connect motifs, colors, and materials to the culture and region that produced them
- Create an original work inspired by folk art without copying sacred or protected imagery
- Discuss how Portinari and Tarsila contributed to Brazilian visual identity at introductory level
- Value community art traditions as part of national cultural heritage

BNCC Skills:
- EF15AR01, EF15AR03, EF15AR25

Keywords:
- folk art
- Brazilian culture
- heritage
- collage
- tradition
- identity

Project Opportunities:
- Folk-art-inspired collage panel celebrating a local festival or tradition
- Research poster on a Brazilian craft region (Northeastern woodcut, Minas Gerais lace, etc.)
- Class mural combining individual folk-motif tiles into a collective composition

Assessment Suggestions:
- Short written response comparing two folk art examples from different regions
- Artwork evaluation with inspiration source and respectful use checklist
- Oral presentation connecting a folk tradition to its community of origin

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Sculpture and integrated studio practice extend two-dimensional skills into space, collaboration, and exhibition.

Topics:
- Introduction to sculpture: additive and subtractive methods with clay, cardboard, and found objects
- Relief, mobile, and simple assemblage structures
- Art systems: museums, galleries, school exhibitions, and the role of the viewer
- Collaborative installation or display planning for end-of-year showcase
- Year review: elements, principles, painting, design, and folk art connections

Learning Objectives:
- Build a stable three-dimensional form considering balance, texture, and negative space
- Distinguish between additive sculpture and carved or cut-away approaches
- Prepare artwork for display with titles, labels, and intentional presentation
- Participate in a group exhibition plan including layout and audience pathway
- Synthesize skills from all four quarters in a culminating personal piece

BNCC Skills:
- EF15AR05, EF15AR06, EF15AR07, EF15AR26 (reinforcement)

Keywords:
- sculpture
- form
- space
- exhibition
- collaboration
- mixed media

Project Opportunities:
- Clay or cardboard sculpture inspired by a Brazilian animal or plant from science link
- Found-object assemblage using recycled materials with written material list
- End-of-year classroom gallery with student-curated wall arrangement

Assessment Suggestions:
- Sculpture stability and craftsmanship rubric with artist statement
- Exhibition planning checklist and peer feedback during setup
- Portfolio review covering one work from each quarter with self-assessment
