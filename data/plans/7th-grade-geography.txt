==================================================

Grade: 7

Subject: Geography

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Seventh-grade geography at Colégio Horizonte examines Brazil's territorial formation, demographic patterns, and connections to global environmental challenges. Students analyze population distribution, migration, economic flows, and cultural diversity across Brazilian regions while developing data literacy through thematic maps, cartograms, and graphs. A light case study of Rio de Janeiro illustrates metropolitan dynamics without dominating the curriculum. US-influenced skills—demographic data interpretation, regional comparison frameworks, and issue-based geographic reasoning—complement BNCC competencies and prepare students for geopolitical analysis in eighth grade.

Core Competencies:
- Analyze demographic patterns, migration, and cultural diversity across Brazilian regions
- Interpret thematic maps, cartograms, and graphs to identify spatial inequalities
- Explain how production, circulation, and consumption shape territories and environments
- Evaluate global environmental issues and connect them to Brazilian contexts

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Brazil's population is unevenly distributed, culturally diverse, and continuously reshaped by migration and economic opportunity.

Topics:
- Stereotypes and media representations of Brazilian landscapes and peoples
- Territorial formation of Brazil: historical flows and contemporary tensions
- Territorial rights of indigenous peoples, quilombola communities, and traditional groups
- Population distribution by region; density, ethnicity, income, sex, and age patterns
- Internal migration routes and push-pull factors within Brazil
- Demographic data sources: IBGE census, thematic maps, and statistical tables

Learning Objectives:
- Critique stereotypes about Brazilian regions using geographic evidence
- Describe major phases and factors in Brazil's territorial formation
- Recognize territorial rights of diverse cultural communities as legal and geographic facts
- Analyze population distribution patterns using IBGE data and thematic maps
- Explain migration flows affecting Rio de Janeiro and other metropolitan centers

Keywords:

Assessment Suggestions:
- Thematic map interpretation with written pattern description

- Essay on territorial rights of one traditional community in Brazil

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Economic systems reorganize Brazilian space through production networks, infrastructure, and uneven development.

Topics:
- From mercantilism to capitalism: historical shifts in economic organization
- Production, circulation, and consumption chains and their environmental impacts
- Transport and communication networks shaping Brazilian territory
- Industrialization, technology, and spatial concentration of economic activity
- Regional economic specialization: agriculture, mining, industry, and services
- Wealth distribution and spatial inequality across Brazilian macro-regions

Learning Objectives:
- Explain how capitalism transformed economic geography from mercantilist patterns
- Trace a consumer product from production to disposal and identify environmental costs
- Analyze how highways, ports, and digital networks connect and rank Brazilian cities
- Relate industrialization to urban growth and regional economic disparities
- Compare economic profiles of two Brazilian regions using multiple data sources

BNCC Skills:
- EF07GE05, EF07GE06, EF07GE07, EF07GE08

Keywords:
- economy
- production
- circulation
- infrastructure
- industrialization
- inequality
- networks

Project Opportunities:
- Supply chain map of a common product showing Brazilian and global connections
- Infrastructure improvement proposal for a underserved region with cost-benefit reasoning
- Regional economic comparison poster using graphs and thematic maps

Assessment Suggestions:
- Supply chain diagram with environmental impact annotations
- Graph interpretation test on regional economic indicators
- Case analysis of how a transport project changed territorial organization

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Global environmental challenges intersect with Brazilian biomes, conservation policy, and local quality of life.

Topics:
- Brazilian biomes in depth: Amazon, Cerrado, Caatinga, Atlantic Forest, Pampa, Pantanal
- Biodiversity threats: deforestation, fires, illegal extraction, and habitat fragmentation
- Conservation units and the SNUC system; comparing local and national protected areas
- Global issues: food security, water scarcity, ocean pollution, and species loss
- Climate variability and extreme events affecting Brazil and the world
- Interpreting environmental data through maps, graphs, and satellite monitoring

Learning Objectives:
- Characterize Brazil's major biomes and their distribution on national maps
- Analyze causes and consequences of biodiversity loss in at least two biomes
- Compare conservation strategies in the school's municipality and another Brazilian location
- Explain how global environmental problems manifest in Brazilian contexts
- Interpret thematic maps and graphs documenting environmental change

BNCC Skills:

Keywords:

- Environmental data dashboard using graphs from IBGE and public agencies (math link)

Assessment Suggestions:
- Biome distribution map test with threat and conservation matching
- Graph and cartogram analysis on environmental indicators
- Policy brief proposing one conservation action at local or national scale

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Metropolitan case studies reveal how demographic, economic, and environmental forces converge in lived urban space.

Topics:
- Rio de Janeiro metropolitan region: morphology, zones, and growth patterns
- Social and spatial inequality: formal neighborhoods, favelas, and risk areas
- Port, tourism, and service economy in a global coastal city
- Environmental pressures: bay pollution, landslides, flooding, and coastal erosion
- Public policy responses: housing, mobility, sanitation, and civil defense
- Integration review: demographics, economy, environment, and urban case analysis

Learning Objectives:
- Describe the spatial structure of metropolitan Rio using maps and zones
- Analyze relationships among income, housing, and environmental risk in urban areas
- Explain how tourism and port activity shape Rio's economy and landscape
- Evaluate environmental challenges affecting Guanabara Bay and hillside communities
- Synthesize seventh-grade themes in a capstone geographic investigation

BNCC Skills:
- EF07GE04, EF07GE06, EF07GE09, EF07GE10 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- Rio de Janeiro

Project Opportunities:

- Neighborhood comparison using demographic and environmental indicators
- Capstone presentation linking a global issue to a Rio-specific example with solutions

Assessment Suggestions:
- Case study examination on Rio's demographic and environmental dynamics
- Thematic map and essay synthesizing three units of seventh-grade content
- Final project rubric assessing data use, spatial reasoning, and evidence-based argument
