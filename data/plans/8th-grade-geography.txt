==================================================

Grade: 8

Subject: Geography

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Eighth-grade geography at Colégio Horizonte centers on geopolitics, sustainability, and climate change within global and Latin American contexts. Students examine population movements, state formation, international organizations, and the geoeconomic influence of the United States and emerging powers on Brazil and neighboring countries. Sustainability and resource management provide a lens for analyzing environmental pressures, urban segregation, and development trade-offs. US geography skills—geopolitical reasoning, regional comparison using socioeconomic indicators, and issue analysis across scales—support BNCC competencies and prepare students for high school humanities and informed citizenship.

Core Competencies:
- Analyze geopolitical relationships among nations, regions, and international organizations
- Evaluate global economic patterns and their environmental and social consequences
- Apply sustainability principles to resource management and territorial planning
- Assess climate change causes, impacts, and responses at local to global scales

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Population movements and political organization shape territories, identities, and international relations across the Americas and Africa.

Topics:
- Historical routes of human dispersal and major migration flows worldwide
- Family and community migration histories linked to global patterns
- Demographic dynamics: age structure, growth rates, and spatial mobility
- Latin American migration: voluntary and forced flows, policies, and border tensions
- Concepts of state, nation, territory, government, and country in contemporary conflicts
- Regional organizations: Mercosul, OEA, Unasul, African Union, and cooperation bodies

Learning Objectives:
- Describe major global migration patterns and their historical and environmental causes
- Connect local family migration stories to continental and global demographic trends
- Analyze demographic indicators and explain their geographic implications
- Compare migration policies and experiences across Latin American countries
- Explain roles of international organizations in regional integration and conflict mediation

BNCC Skills:
- EF08GE01, EF08GE02, EF08GE03, EF08GE04, EF08GE05, EF08GE06, EF08GE11, EF08GE12

Keywords:
- migration
- demographics
- geopolitics
- state
- territory
- organizations
- Latin America

Project Opportunities:
- Migration story atlas linking student family histories to global flow maps
- International organization briefing on one body's goals and regional impact
- Border conflict case study with map showing tensions and cooperation efforts

Assessment Suggestions:
- Migration flow map with factor analysis written component
- Demographic data interpretation using population pyramids and mobility statistics
- Structured debate on a regional cooperation challenge in Latin America

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Global economic and political power reshapes territories through competition, integration, and uneven development.

Topics:
- United States geopolitical and geoeconomic influence in the postwar order
- China, BRICS nations, and shifting global economic leadership
- Brazil's position in Latin America and relations with major world powers
- Worldwide patterns of agricultural and industrial production and trade
- Social movements in rural and urban Brazil compared with Latin American examples
- Economic decentralization and recentralization driven by US and Chinese capital

Learning Objectives:
- Analyze US global leadership and its economic, strategic, and political dimensions
- Explain BRICS cooperation and competition in global production networks
- Evaluate Brazil's role in Latin American trade, diplomacy, and resource politics
- Compare social movements addressing land, labor, and urban rights across countries
- Interpret how multinational investment reorganizes economic activity across regions

BNCC Skills:
- EF08GE07, EF08GE08, EF08GE09, EF08GE10, EF08GE13, EF08GE14

Keywords:
- geopolitics
- United States
- BRICS
- trade
- social movements
- power
- development

Project Opportunities:
- Geoeconomic analysis poster: one global commodity chain involving Brazil
- Comparative study of urban and rural social movements in two Latin American countries
- Model UN-style simulation on a trade or environmental dispute involving Brazil

Assessment Suggestions:
- Essay comparing US and Chinese influence on a Latin American economic sector
- Trade flow map interpretation with written analysis of dependencies
- Case study test on a Brazilian social movement and its regional parallels

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Sustainable territorial management requires balancing resource use, social equity, and environmental protection across scales.

Topics:
- Latin American water resources: Guarani Aquifer, Amazon and Plata basins, and management conflicts
- Natural resources and energy production in Latin America; Mercosul cooperation
- Urban problems in major Latin American cities: housing, services, and employment
- Socio-spatial segregation: favelas, informal settlements, and risk zones
- Cartographic analysis: thematic maps, croquis, cartograms, and anamorphosis
- Light Rio reference: water management, urban inequality, and hillside risk planning

Learning Objectives:
- Analyze significance and governance challenges of major Latin American water systems
- Evaluate resource extraction and energy production trade-offs in regional development
- Compare living and working conditions in large Latin American metropolitan areas
- Explain socio-spatial segregation patterns using maps, data, and case examples
- Create and interpret cartographic representations of urban-rural dynamics

- Cartogram or thematic map project on IDH, sanitation, or income in Latin America
- Sustainability plan for a Latin American city addressing water and housing challenges

Assessment Suggestions:
- Cartographic skills test: croquis, cartogram, and thematic map interpretation
- Urban inequality analysis using socioeconomic indicators and spatial description

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Climate change is a defining geographic challenge requiring scientific understanding, international cooperation, and local action.

Topics:
- Greenhouse effect, carbon cycle, and evidence of global climate change
- Climate impacts on Latin America and Africa: drought, floods, sea level, and biodiversity
- Antarctica's environmental and geopolitical significance for South America
- Technology, production systems, and their role in environmental transformation
- International climate agreements and national mitigation and adaptation strategies
- Brazil's climate policy, Amazon protection, and renewable energy transitions
- Integration review: geopolitics, sustainability, and climate action across scales

Learning Objectives:
- Explain causes and evidence of climate change using scientific and geographic sources
- Analyze climate impacts on populations, economies, and ecosystems in multiple regions
- Discuss Antarctica's role in global climate research and territorial politics
- Evaluate mitigation and adaptation strategies at municipal, national, and global levels
- Propose evidence-based climate actions connecting global frameworks to local practice

BNCC Skills:
- EF08GE20, EF08GE21, EF08GE08 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:

Project Opportunities:

- Climate justice presentation comparing impacts on vulnerable regions globally
- Capstone policy forum: students represent nations negotiating a climate action proposal

Assessment Suggestions:
- Climate change cause-impact-response exam with map and graph components
- Position paper on one Brazilian climate policy with geopolitical context
- End-of-year synthesis project linking geopolitics, sustainability, and climate across units
