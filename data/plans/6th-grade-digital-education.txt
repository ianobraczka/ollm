==================================================

Grade: 6

Subject: Digital Education

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Sixth-grade digital education at Colégio Horizonte strengthens students' ability to find, evaluate, and communicate information in digital environments while introducing computational thinking as a problem-solving approach. Building on fifth-grade citizenship foundations, students practice research skills for science and humanities projects, collaborate through school-approved platforms, and decompose problems into logical steps. The curriculum supports interdisciplinary work by teaching tools and habits that transfer directly to math data projects and science investigations.

Core Competencies:
- Locate and evaluate digital information using credibility criteria
- Communicate ideas effectively through appropriate online channels and formats
- Apply computational thinking strategies to break problems into manageable steps
- Collaborate on shared documents while maintaining version control and attribution

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Information literacy begins with purposeful searching and critical evaluation of sources.

Topics:
- Research question refinement and keyword selection
- Using library databases, encyclopedias, and curated school resources
- Evaluating credibility: author, date, purpose, evidence, bias
- Distinguishing fact, opinion, and sponsored content
- Note-taking methods and citation basics for school projects

Learning Objectives:
- Formulate effective search queries for a defined research question
- Apply a source evaluation checklist to online and database materials
- Identify bias and missing evidence in sample articles
- Record notes in a structured format with source references
- Explain why Wikipedia is a starting point but not a final source

BNCC Skills:
- EF06CO01, EF06CO02, EF06CO03

Keywords:
- information literacy
- investigation
- ethics
- communication
- critical thinking
- research

Project Opportunities:
- Endangered species research dossier supporting science presentations
- Source comparison activity on a current environmental topic
- Class bibliography wall with rated sources and justification notes

Assessment Suggestions:
- Source evaluation worksheet with written credibility ratings
- Annotated bibliography for a science or humanities topic
- Short test on fact vs. opinion in sample web excerpts

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Online communication requires clarity, audience awareness, and norms that differ from face-to-face interaction.

Topics:
- Email and messaging etiquette for school contexts
- Discussion boards and comment norms: constructive feedback
- Video call participation: mute, camera, and sharing protocols
- Digital collaboration on shared documents and slide decks
- Managing notifications and maintaining focus during online work

Learning Objectives:
- Write clear, appropriately formal school emails with subject lines and signatures
- Post constructive comments that advance group learning
- Follow video conference norms that respect all participants
- Edit shared documents without deleting others' contributions
- Balance responsiveness with focused work time

Project Opportunities:

- Participation log and self-assessment for online collaboration
- Revision task showing tracked changes and response to feedback

==================================================

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Computational thinking organizes complex problems through decomposition, patterns, abstraction, and algorithms.

Topics:
- Decomposition: breaking projects into subtasks with timelines
- Pattern recognition in data, nature, and daily routines
- Abstraction: focusing on essential details while ignoring noise
- Algorithms: step-by-step instructions and flowchart notation
- Introduction to block-based programming environments approved by the school

Learning Objectives:
- Decompose a multi-step project into ordered subtasks with dependencies
- Identify repeating patterns in numerical and natural data sets
- Create a flowchart representing a decision process or recipe
- Write an algorithm in plain language that another person can follow
- Build a simple interactive program using block-based coding

- Flowchart accuracy check against a given process description
- Algorithm execution test: classmate follows steps without clarification
- Programming project rubric covering functionality and documentation

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Digital skills integrate with subject learning when students publish research, data, and solutions responsibly.

Topics:
- Spreadsheet skills: sorting, filtering, basic formulas
- Chart creation from science and math data sets
- Presentation design for oral defense of projects
- Digital rights, Creative Commons, and plagiarism avoidance
- Year-end technology reflection and goal setting

Learning Objectives:
- Organize survey data in a spreadsheet with consistent formatting
- Create appropriate chart types for categorical and numerical data
- Design a presentation that supports—not replaces—oral explanation
- Apply citation and licensing rules to all published digital work
- Set measurable goals for seventh-grade technology competencies

Assessment Suggestions:
- Spreadsheet and chart accuracy review against raw data
- Presentation rubric with design, content, and citation criteria
- Portfolio reflection linking digital skills to interdisciplinary projects
