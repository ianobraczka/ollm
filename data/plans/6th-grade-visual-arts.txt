Grade: 6

Subject: Visual Arts

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Sixth-grade visual arts at Colégio Horizonte deepens technical skill through perspective drawing, printmaking, and critical study of contemporary Brazilian artists. Students move beyond foundational elements to explore how artists represent space, reproduce images through print processes, and respond to modern society. Studio work emphasizes individual and collaborative creation, contextual research, and ethical use of materials. Connections to geography and social studies enrich discussions of art as a lens on Brazilian identity, urban life, and cultural diversity.

Core Competencies:
- Apply one-point and introductory two-point perspective in architectural and interior drawings
- Produce original prints using relief and monoprint techniques with intentional repetition and variation
- Research and analyze works by contemporary Brazilian visual artists in historical and social context
- Develop personal creative processes from research through revision and presentation

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Perspective systems allow artists to create the illusion of depth and place viewers inside imagined spaces.

Topics:
- One-point perspective: horizon line, vanishing point, and converging lines
- Two-point perspective introduction for corners, buildings, and street scenes
- Atmospheric perspective: size, value, and detail changes with distance
- Urban and rural Brazilian streetscapes as drawing subjects
- Sketchbook practice: quick perspective studies from classroom photos and field views

Learning Objectives:
- Construct a one-point perspective grid and place objects correctly in space
- Draw a building corner using two vanishing points with consistent line direction
- Use value and scale changes to suggest depth without full perspective grids
- Apply perspective to original scenes rather than only copying templates
- Critique perspective accuracy in peer drawings using constructive vocabulary

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR04, EF69AR05, EF69AR06

Keywords:
- perspective
- depth
- drawing
- space
- urban landscape
- proportion

Project Opportunities:
- Brazilian city street drawing in one-point perspective with human figures for scale
- Before-and-after sketchbook page showing perspective correction through revision
- Collaborative panoramic mural of an imagined town square (social studies link)

Assessment Suggestions:
- Perspective construction quiz with drawn corrections
- Rubric evaluating vanishing point accuracy, detail, and composition
- Written reflection on challenges of representing three-dimensional space on paper

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Printmaking multiplies images and invites artists to explore pattern, texture, and serial variation.

Topics:
- Relief printing: foam plate, linoleum introduction, and cardboard stamp carving
- Monoprint and trace monotype for unique impressions
- Registration, ink application, and paper choice for clean editions
- Pattern, repetition, and motif design in Brazilian textile and popular print traditions
- Editioning concepts: artist proofs, numbered prints, and signing conventions

Learning Objectives:
- Carve or incise a relief plate safely with clear positive and negative areas
- Pull at least three consistent prints from a single plate with even inking
- Create a monoprint series exploring one theme with controlled variation
- Explain how printmaking differs from unique drawing and painting
- Design a repeating motif suitable for fabric, wallpaper, or poster application

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR05, EF69AR06, EF69AR07

Keywords:
- printmaking
- relief
- monoprint
- pattern
- repetition
- texture

Project Opportunities:
- Linoleum or soft-block print edition on a Brazilian nature or cultural theme
- Monoprint landscape series inspired by a biome studied in science
- Class print exchange: trade prints and assemble a curated hallway display

Assessment Suggestions:
- Print edition evaluation for consistency, craftsmanship, and composition
- Process journal documenting plate development, test prints, and revisions
- Short comparison essay: printmaking vs. digital reproduction (digital education link)

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Contemporary Brazilian artists use diverse media and ideas to question society, identity, and the role of art today.

Topics:
- Contemporary art contexts: galleries, street art, biennials, and digital platforms
- Brazilian artists and movements: neo-concrete echoes, contemporary painting, photography, and installation
- Candido Portinari and Tarsila do Amaral revisited as bridges between modern and contemporary Brazil
- Art analysis framework: subject, medium, technique, context, and interpretation
- Social themes in art: inequality, environment, migration, and youth culture

Learning Objectives:
- Research a contemporary Brazilian artist and present key works with dates and media
- Analyze an artwork using description, analysis, interpretation, and judgment steps
- Compare how historical and contemporary artists address Brazilian identity differently
- Discuss street art and gallery art as valid but distinct cultural spaces
- Generate original concepts responding to a contemporary social issue through visual means

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR01, EF69AR02, EF69AR33, EF69AR34

Keywords:
- contemporary art
- Brazilian artists
- analysis
- identity
- context
- interpretation

Project Opportunities:
- Artist research dossier with reproductions and annotated commentary
- Concept sketch for an installation or mural addressing a local community topic
- Classroom debate and written response on art in public versus museum spaces

Assessment Suggestions:
- Art analysis worksheet applied to a contemporary Brazilian work
- Research presentation rubric for accuracy, visuals, and critical insight
- Concept proposal with thumbnails and artist statement draft

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Integrated studio practice combines perspective, printmaking, and contemporary awareness into sustained personal projects.

Topics:
- Cross-media project development: drawing, painting, print, and collage in one theme
- Professional roles in visual arts: artist, curator, designer, and art educator introductions
- Portfolio organization: selecting, photographing, and labeling best work
- Collaborative curation of a grade-level mini-exhibition
- Year synthesis: technical growth, cultural knowledge, and creative risk-taking

Learning Objectives:
- Plan and complete a multi-step project with documented research and revisions
- Articulate influences from folk, modern, and contemporary Brazilian art in personal work
- Photograph artwork adequately for digital portfolio records
- Assist in arranging an exhibition with titles, labels, and audience flow
- Set one technical and one conceptual goal for seventh-grade visual arts

BNCC Skills:
- EF69AR06, EF69AR07, EF69AR08, EF69AR31 (reinforcement)

Keywords:
- portfolio
- curation
- mixed media
- exhibition
- reflection
- synthesis

Project Opportunities:
- Themed mixed-media series combining perspective drawing and print elements
- Student-curated hallway show with written curatorial statement
- Digital portfolio folder with photographed works and process pages (digital education link)

Assessment Suggestions:
- Multi-week project rubric covering research, technique, originality, and presentation
- Curatorial statement evaluating clarity of theme and artwork selection
- End-of-year self-assessment against core competencies and personal goals
