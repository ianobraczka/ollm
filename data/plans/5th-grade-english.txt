Grade: 5

Subject: English

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Fifth-grade English at Colégio Horizonte builds near-native fluency through immersive literature circles and narrative study. Students read age-appropriate chapter books and short fiction, discussing plot, character, and theme in sustained English conversation. Rio de Janeiro and Brazilian settings appear alongside international texts, helping students see themselves as bilingual readers who belong to a global literary community while staying rooted in local culture.

Core Competencies:
- Read narrative texts independently with comprehension strategies and expressive fluency
- Participate in literature circles using evidence from the text to support ideas
- Identify and discuss story elements: setting, character, conflict, and resolution
- Write organized narrative pieces with clear beginnings, middles, and endings

==================================================

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Readers construct meaning from narrative texts by noticing how authors organize story elements.

Topics:
- Reading workshop routines: independent reading, conferring, and response journals
- Story elements: setting, characters, point of view, and plot structure
- Making predictions, connections, and inferences while reading
- Vocabulary in context: context clues, cognates, and word families
- Introductory texts with Rio and international settings (e.g., city life, school, family)

Learning Objectives:
- Establish daily independent reading habits with self-selected books at appropriate level
- Identify and describe setting, main characters, and central conflict in a narrative
- Use text evidence to support predictions and personal connections
- Record reading responses in a journal with dates, titles, and brief reflections
- Discuss unfamiliar words using context and bilingual dictionary strategies

BNCC Skills:
- EF06LI07, EF06LI08, EF06LI09, EF06LI10, EF06LI12

Keywords:
- narrative
- reading workshop
- story elements
- inference
- vocabulary
- comprehension

Project Opportunities:
- Personal reading log with genre tracking and mini-reviews
- Story map poster for a class read-aloud set in Brazil or abroad
- Vocabulary word wall built from shared texts and student discoveries

Assessment Suggestions:
- Reading conference rubric evaluating fluency, comprehension, and text talk
- Story elements graphic organizer with written evidence from the text
- Vocabulary quiz using words from current unit texts in context

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Literature circles deepen understanding when readers share perspectives and question the text together.

Topics:
- Literature circle roles: discussion director, connector, word wizard, summarizer
- Character traits, motivations, and change across a story
- Dialogue and quotation marks: reading and writing spoken language
- Shared novel or short story cycle with rotating small-group discussions
- Comparing characters from different cultural backgrounds and experiences

Learning Objectives:
- Prepare for literature circle meetings with assigned role and text notes
- Ask open-ended questions that invite classmates to cite evidence
- Describe how a character's actions reveal traits and motivations
- Track character change from beginning to end of a text
- Listen actively and build on peers' ideas in English discussion

BNCC Skills:
- EF06LI01, EF06LI02, EF06LI03, EF06LI04, EF06LI12

Keywords:
- literature circles
- character
- discussion
- evidence
- dialogue
- collaboration

Project Opportunities:
- Character profile dossier with quotes, illustrations, and trait analysis
- Recorded literature circle podcast episode on a class novel chapter
- Cross-text comparison chart: two protagonists facing similar challenges

Assessment Suggestions:
- Literature circle participation checklist and self-reflection
- Character analysis paragraph with embedded quotations
- Oral presentation: "What would this character notice walking through Rio?"

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Writers craft narratives by shaping scenes, voice, and sequence to engage readers.

Topics:
- Narrative structure: hook, rising action, climax, and resolution
- Show, don't tell: sensory details and strong verbs
- Writing personal narratives and realistic fiction inspired by student experience
- Revision strategies: adding detail, varying sentence length, peer feedback
- Publishing formats: class anthology, digital story, or illustrated booklet

Learning Objectives:
- Plan a narrative using a story mountain or timeline organizer
- Write scenes with dialogue, action, and descriptive detail
- Revise drafts for clarity, organization, and word choice
- Edit for capitalization, punctuation, and paragraph breaks
- Share finished narratives in author chair sessions with audience questions

BNCC Skills:
- EF06LI13, EF06LI14, EF06LI15, EF06LI19, EF06LI20

Keywords:
- narrative writing
- revision
- voice
- sensory detail
- dialogue
- publishing

Project Opportunities:
- Illustrated personal narrative about a meaningful day in Rio or travel memory
- Class anthology: "Stories from Horizonte" with cover design and author bios
- Partner revision workshop with structured feedback forms

Assessment Suggestions:
- Narrative writing rubric: structure, development, conventions, and voice
- Before-and-after revision sample showing intentional changes
- Author chair reflection on writing choices and audience response

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Reflective readers connect themes across texts and celebrate growth as members of a reading community.

Topics:
- Theme: identifying big ideas about friendship, courage, identity, and belonging
- Author study: style, recurring topics, and craft moves in one writer's work
- Genre exploration: realistic fiction, adventure, and folktales from diverse traditions
- End-of-year reading goals and next-level book recommendations
- Integration review: story elements, character, theme, and narrative craft

Learning Objectives:
- State a theme supported by multiple details from a text
- Compare how two texts address a similar theme in different ways
- Describe an author's craft choices: repetition, figurative language, chapter endings
- Set summer reading goals with annotated book list and rationale
- Synthesize learning from the year in a reading portfolio or letter to future self

BNCC Skills:
- EF06LI11, EF06LI24, EF06LI25, EF06LI26 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- theme
- author study
- genre
- reflection
- portfolio
- reading community

Project Opportunities:
- Author study display with timeline, quotes, and student mimicry paragraphs
- Book recommendation fair for fourth graders: pitches with trailers or posters
- Year-end reading portfolio with best journal entries, writing, and goal setting

Assessment Suggestions:
- Theme essay or paragraph comparing two texts with cited evidence
- Author study presentation with examples of craft moves
- End-of-year reading portfolio evaluated for growth and reflection depth
