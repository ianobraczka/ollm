==================================================

Grade: 5

Subject: Geography

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Fifth-grade geography at Colégio Horizonte builds spatial literacy through map skills, regional study of Brazil, and introductory climate analysis. Students learn to read and create maps, identify Brazil's five macro-regions and their physical characteristics, and connect climate patterns to landscapes and daily life. Field observations in the school neighborhood and comparisons with other Brazilian regions anchor abstract cartographic concepts in places students know. US-influenced geography skills—cardinal directions, scale, legend interpretation, and thematic map analysis—support BNCC competencies while keeping Brazil as the primary frame of reference.

Core Competencies:
- Read and create maps using scale, legend, coordinates, and thematic layers
- Describe Brazil's macro-regions, major landforms, and representative climates
- Explain relationships among climate, vegetation, and human settlement patterns
- Identify local environmental issues and connect them to regional contexts

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Maps are tools for representing, measuring, and interpreting Earth's features at different scales.

Topics:
- Purpose and types of maps: political, physical, thematic, and satellite imagery
- Map elements: title, legend, scale (graphic and numeric), orientation, and coordinates
- Cardinal and intermediate directions; locating places on Brazil and world maps
- Latitude and longitude at introductory level; parallels and meridians
- Reading elevation and landform symbols; simple sketch maps of the school area
- Comparing paper maps with digital maps and aerial photographs

Learning Objectives:
- Identify essential map components and explain what each communicates
- Use scale to estimate distances between cities within Rio de Janeiro state
- Locate Brazil, its capital, and the school's municipality on world and national maps
- Construct a simple map of the school neighborhood with a legend and scale bar
- Compare how the same place appears on a topographic map, photo, and satellite image

BNCC Skills:
- EF05GE08, EF05GE09

Keywords:
- maps
- scale
- legend
- coordinates
- spatial thinking
- cartography
- orientation

Project Opportunities:
- Neighborhood sketch map with landmarks, routes, and a written scale statement
- Map scavenger hunt using Brazil political and physical classroom atlases
- Before-and-after satellite image comparison of a growing area in Greater Rio

Assessment Suggestions:
- Map elements labeling and function explanation quiz
- Distance estimation exercise using graphic scale on a Rio state road map
- Peer review of sketch maps for accuracy, clarity, and complete legends

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
Brazil's territory is organized into distinct regions shaped by physical geography, history, and economic activity.

Topics:
- Brazil's five macro-regions: North, Northeast, Center-West, Southeast, and South
- Major landforms: Amazon Basin, Brazilian Highlands, Pantanal, coastal plains, and plateaus
- Rivers and hydrographic basins: Amazon, São Francisco, Paraná, and Tietê references
- Population distribution patterns across regions; Rio de Janeiro in the Southeast
- Economic activities by region: agriculture, mining, industry, services, and tourism
- City-countryside relationships and urban networks connecting Brazilian cities

Learning Objectives:
- Name and locate Brazil's five macro-regions on a political map
- Associate each region with key landforms, rivers, and economic activities
- Describe why population and cities concentrate in the Southeast and coastal areas
- Compare the school's region with one contrasting region (e.g., North or Northeast)
- Explain how transportation links cities within and between macro-regions

BNCC Skills:
- EF05GE01, EF05GE02, EF05GE04, EF05GE05, EF05GE06

Keywords:
- Brazil
- regions
- landforms
- population
- economy
- urban networks
- territory

Project Opportunities:
- Regional profile poster: one macro-region with map, symbols, and economic facts
- Class atlas page compiling student research on each Brazilian region
- Data table comparing population density across regions (math link)

Assessment Suggestions:
- Regional map quiz with landform and economic activity matching
- Short essay comparing two Brazilian regions using geographic vocabulary
- Oral presentation on how Rio de Janeiro connects to other cities in the Southeast

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Climate influences landscapes, ecosystems, and how people live across Brazil's vast territory.

Topics:
- Weather versus climate; measuring temperature and rainfall over time
- Climate zones in Brazil: equatorial, tropical, semi-arid, subtropical, and highland
- Relationship among climate, soil, vegetation, and biome characteristics
- Brazilian biomes overview: Amazon, Cerrado, Caatinga, Atlantic Forest, Pampa, Pantanal
- Seasonal patterns and the influence of latitude, altitude, and proximity to the ocean
- Climate and daily life: clothing, housing, agriculture, and water availability

Learning Objectives:
- Distinguish daily weather from long-term climate patterns
- Identify major climate types in Brazil and associate them with regions on a map
- Explain how climate affects vegetation and land use in at least three biomes
- Describe Rio de Janeiro's tropical climate and its seasonal rainfall pattern
- Interpret simple climate graphs comparing a coastal and an interior Brazilian city

BNCC Skills:
- EF05GE03, EF05GE07, EF05GE10

Keywords:
- climate
- weather
- biomes
- vegetation
- rainfall
- temperature
- environment

Project Opportunities:
- Climate graph construction for Rio de Janeiro and one other Brazilian city (math link)
- Biome collage with climate description, native species, and human land use
- Weather journal with monthly summaries linking observations to seasonal patterns

Assessment Suggestions:
- Climate zone map labeling with biome matching
- Climate graph interpretation worksheet
- Written explanation of how climate shapes life in a chosen Brazilian biome

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Geographic understanding empowers students to recognize environmental challenges and participate in community solutions.

Topics:
- Environmental quality indicators: clean water, air, green spaces, and waste management
- Local environmental problems near the school: litter, flooding, erosion, and pollution
- Water resources: rivers, reservoirs, and ocean health along the Rio coastline
- Urban growth and landscape change in metropolitan Rio de Janeiro
- Public institutions and citizen participation for environmental improvement
- Year review: integrating maps, regions, climate, and local place-based knowledge

Learning Objectives:
- Identify environmental problems in the school surroundings and propose solutions
- Explain how urban growth alters landscapes using local and satellite examples
- Describe how communities can participate in environmental decision-making
- Connect regional climate and land use to environmental risks such as flooding
- Synthesize map skills, regional knowledge, and climate concepts in a capstone project

BNCC Skills:
- EF05GE08, EF05GE10, EF05GE11, EF05GE12 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- environment
- sustainability
- urban growth
- citizenship
- water
- pollution
- community

Project Opportunities:
- School environmental audit with map showing problem locations and proposed fixes
- Letter or presentation to local authorities proposing one feasible improvement
- End-of-year geography fair booth: maps, regional display, and climate exhibit

Assessment Suggestions:
- Environmental action plan evaluated for geographic reasoning and feasibility
- Concept map linking units: maps, regions, climate, and local environment
- Portfolio review of map work, regional research, and climate investigations
