Grade: 6

Subject: Geography

Academic Year: 2025

Overview:
Sixth-grade geography at Colégio Horizonte deepens understanding of how human societies transform landscapes, with emphasis on urbanization and environmental dynamics in Brazil. Students analyze the rise of cities, changing land use, and the physical-natural systems—climate, water, and vegetation—that shape territorial organization. Inquiry methods drawn from US geography education—spatial pattern analysis, evidence-based argument, and layered map interpretation—strengthen BNCC competencies. Local observations in the Rio de Janeiro metropolitan area connect national processes to places students experience daily.

Core Competencies:
- Analyze how urbanization and economic activity transform natural and cultural landscapes
- Explain interactions among climate, water systems, soil, and vegetation in Brazil
- Apply cartographic tools including scale, profiles, and thematic maps to measure space
- Evaluate human environmental impacts and compare mitigation strategies across contexts

Unit 1

Period: First Quarter

Big Idea:
Landscapes are living records of how societies use, modify, and assign meaning to space over time.

Topics:
- Concept of landscape: natural, cultural, and built features
- Comparing landscape change in places of student experience across time periods
- Indigenous and traditional societies' relationships with Brazilian landscapes
- Agricultural and extractive activities reshaping rural environments
- Industrialization and infrastructure as drivers of landscape transformation
- Photographs, maps, and satellite images as evidence of landscape change

Learning Objectives:
- Define landscape and identify natural and human-made elements in local examples
- Compare historical and contemporary images to describe landscape transformations
- Explain how different societies, including indigenous peoples, shape environments
- Analyze how agriculture and mining alter vegetation, soil, and water systems
- Use visual sources to support claims about environmental change in Brazil

BNCC Skills:
- EF06GE01, EF06GE02, EF06GE06

Keywords:
- landscape
- transformation
- indigenous peoples
- agriculture
- industrialization
- evidence
- territory

Project Opportunities:
- Then-and-now landscape study of a Rio neighborhood using photos and maps
- Timeline display of landscape change in one Brazilian biome
- Interview-based local history project on how the school area has changed

Assessment Suggestions:
- Image analysis worksheet identifying landscape change and causes
- Short essay on indigenous territorial perspectives in a Brazilian region
- Map-based presentation comparing rural and urban landscape features

Unit 2

Period: Second Quarter

Big Idea:
The growth of cities reconfigures human-nature relationships and creates new environmental opportunities and risks.

Topics:
- Origins and expansion of cities; rural-urban migration in Brazil
- Urban functions: residential, commercial, industrial, and service zones
- Metropolitan areas and the Rio de Janeiro urban agglomeration
- Urban infrastructure: housing, transport, sanitation, and energy networks
- Social and environmental consequences of rapid urbanization
- City planning concepts: zoning, green spaces, and public participation

Learning Objectives:
- Explain factors that drive urban growth and migration to Brazilian cities
- Describe functions of urban zones and how they appear on city maps
- Analyze challenges of metropolitan expansion in the Rio de Janeiro region
- Compare quality-of-life indicators between urban center and periphery
- Propose planning ideas that balance development with environmental protection

BNCC Skills:
- EF06GE07, EF06GE11, EF06GE13

Keywords:
- urbanization
- cities
- migration
- infrastructure
- planning
- metropolitan area
- environment

Project Opportunities:
- Urban land-use map of a Rio district with zone classification and legend
- Case study poster on one major Brazilian city and its growth challenges
- Debate on sustainable urban development using evidence from Rio and Brasília

Assessment Suggestions:
- Urban growth cause-and-effect diagram with Brazilian examples
- Infrastructure gap analysis comparing two neighborhoods
- Written proposal for improving one urban environmental issue locally

Unit 3

Period: Third Quarter

Big Idea:
Physical-natural systems organize Brazilian territory and condition how societies use water, land, and climate.

Topics:
- Earth movements and their influence on atmospheric circulation and climate
- Climate patterns across Brazil: temperature, rainfall, and seasonal variation
- Water cycle; surface runoff in urban and rural settings
- River basins, watershed morphology, and Brazil's major hydrographic systems
- Relationships among climate, soil, relief, and vegetation formations
- Map scale: measuring distances with graphic and numeric scales

Learning Objectives:
- Describe how atmospheric circulation produces Brazil's climate patterns
- Explain the water cycle and compare runoff in urban and rural environments
- Locate major Brazilian river basins and explain their territorial significance
- Relate climate, relief, soil, and vegetation in at least three regional examples
- Measure distances on maps using scale and estimate real-world distances

BNCC Skills:
- EF06GE03, EF06GE04, EF06GE05, EF06GE08, EF06GE12

Keywords:
- climate
- water cycle
- river basins
- relief
- vegetation
- scale
- physical geography

Project Opportunities:
- Three-dimensional model or block diagram of a river basin near Rio de Janeiro
- Climate-relief-vegetation chart for three Brazilian locations
- Water use investigation: school consumption and Guandu Basin context

Assessment Suggestions:
- Basin mapping and labeling test with scale distance calculations
- Climate-vegetation relationship explanation using regional examples
- Lab-style report on a water cycle demonstration with urban-rural comparison

Unit 4

Period: Fourth Quarter

Big Idea:
Responsible territorial management requires understanding environmental impacts and evaluating choices for Brazil's future.

Topics:
- Land use practices: crop rotation, terracing, irrigation, and conservation
- Water resource management: supply, treatment, distribution, and consumption
- Biodiversity and human impacts on ecosystems across Brazilian biomes
- Urban heat islands and microclimate changes from human activity
- Environmental consequences and trade-offs of development projects
- Integration review: urbanization, physical systems, and sustainability in Brazil

Learning Objectives:
- Compare land and water management techniques and their advantages/disadvantages
- Analyze how human activities affect biodiversity in local and national contexts
- Explain urban heat island effects and identify causes in metropolitan areas
- Evaluate environmental trade-offs in a development scenario with evidence
- Synthesize landscape, urban, and physical geography learning in a final inquiry

BNCC Skills:
- EF06GE09, EF06GE10, EF06GE11, EF06GE13 (reinforcement and integration)

Keywords:
- sustainability
- water management
- biodiversity
- land use
- urban heat
- trade-offs
- Brazil

Project Opportunities:
- Sustainability action plan for the school addressing water, waste, or green space
- Environmental impact assessment of a hypothetical project in the Rio periphery
- Cross-unit exhibition: landscapes, cities, physical systems, and student solutions

Assessment Suggestions:
- Land and water management comparison matrix with Brazilian case studies
- Full inquiry report on a local environmental issue with maps and data
- End-of-year concept map connecting urbanization and environment across Brazil
